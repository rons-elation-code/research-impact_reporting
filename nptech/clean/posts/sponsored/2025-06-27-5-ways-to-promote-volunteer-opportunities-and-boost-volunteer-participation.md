# 5 Ways to Promote Your Volunteer Opportunities and Boost Volunteer Participation

> If nonprofits or community organizations want to grow their volunteer base, they must actively market their opportunities in smart, effective ways that inspire action. This post features five proven strategies to help nonprofits market volunteer opportunities and recruit passionate individuals dedicated to driving their missions forward.

Volunteers are a vital resource for nonprofit organizations. They bring energy, passion, and dedication to causes that demand more hands and hearts than paid staffing can provide. But acquiring and retaining committed volunteers is challenging, and standing out in the crowd requires more than a simple call for help. If nonprofits or community organizations want to grow their volunteer base, they must actively market their opportunities in smart, effective ways that inspire action.

Below are five proven strategies to help nonprofit organizations market volunteer opportunities and recruit passionate individuals dedicated to driving their missions forward.

## **1) Craft a Compelling Volunteer Story**

Stories move people to act, especially when they tug at heartstrings or ignite a personal connection. [A compelling narrative](https://bloomerang.co/blog/the-quick-and-no-nonsense-guide-to-nonprofit-storytelling-4-steps-for-the-busy-nonprofit-professional/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all) goes beyond listing tasks or requirements for volunteers. It paints a vivid picture of the difference volunteers make and shows the transformation their contributions create. In addition, when a good story is shared, scientific research shows the direct effect storytelling has on listeners:

- Stories are remembered [22x](https://www.forbes.com/sites/kateharrison/2015/01/20/a-good-presentation-is-about-data-and-story/?sh=6a15222a450f) more easily than data, facts, and stats
- Neural activity [increases 5X](http://www.alsinapublishing.com/2017/11/the-science-of-storytelling/) when listening to a story
- Storytelling [engages the sensory cortex in the brain](http://www.alsinapublishing.com/2017/11/the-science-of-storytelling/), allowing the listener to feel, hear, taste, and even smell the story

When nonprofits share their calls to action, they should emphasize the human impact of their work. For example, rather than stating, “Volunteers are needed to tutor children,” the request could be framed as, “Spend just two hours a week mentoring a child to help them build confidence and thrive academically and personally.” Personal testimonials, statistics, photos, and videos can further legitimize and enhance the emotional appeal.

People don’t volunteer simply to complete tasks. They are motivated by a desire to be part of something bigger than themselves. By showing the “why” behind each opportunity, nonprofits can transform casual interest into passionate commitment.

**Pro Tip:** Featuring [firsthand accounts](https://bloomerang.co/blog/a-beginners-guide-to-conducting-great-nonprofit-storytelling-interviews-6-tips-for-success/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all) from existing volunteers can inspire others. Sharing their stories allows potential recruits to see the tangible rewards of volunteering.

## **2) Meet Volunteers Where They Are**

Marketing volunteer opportunities aren’t just about what’s said or the story that’s told. It’s also about *where* a story is told. To reach potential volunteers, nonprofit professionals need to [meet them where they spend their time](https://bloomerang.co/blog/volunteer-recruitment-plan/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all).

Social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn are powerful tools for sharing opportunities and connecting with people who care about a nonprofit’s mission. On Instagram, for instance, posting behind-the-scenes photos of volunteers in action can create emotional resonance. On LinkedIn, emphasizing the professional skills volunteers can gain can appeal to ambitious, community-focused professionals.

Offline channels are equally significant. Flyers, booths at local events, partnerships with community businesses, and bulletin board announcements can reach audiences less active on digital platforms.

Tailoring outreach efforts to specific audience demographics amplifies engagement rates. For instance, younger individuals might resonate with TikTok videos or Instagram Stories, while retirees might prefer traditional newsletters or community newspaper ads.

**Pro Tip:** Social media geotargeting is an effective technique to attract locals. Adding location tags to posts increases visibility among nearby individuals.

## **3) Simplify the Sign-Up Process**

A complicated sign-up process can be a significant barrier for potential volunteers. When individuals encounter overly long forms, unclear steps, or complicated instructions, their initial excitement can quickly fade, causing them to [abandon the process](https://bloomerang.co/blog/donor-abandonment-what-it-is-and-how-to-avoid-it/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all) and move on. For nonprofits, this not only results in lost opportunities but also undermines the effectiveness of their volunteer marketing efforts.

Nonprofits should ensure that signing up to volunteer is seamless and straightforward. Clear calls-to-action (CTAs) should direct potential volunteers to user-friendly landing pages. These pages must outline the requirements, describe what volunteers can expect, and explain how their involvement will make an impact. Accommodating various preferences, such as offering sign-up via email, text, or web forms, further enhances accessibility.

A well-designed sign-up experience also has a ripple effect. Individuals who find the process easy and welcoming are more likely to share their positive impressions with friends and family, further amplifying the organization’s outreach. Additionally, the seamless interaction reassures potential volunteers that they are engaging with a capable and organized team, reinforcing the nonprofit’s mission and credibility.

For recurring volunteer needs, nonprofits can streamline the application process for those who want to sign up regularly with the help of [volunteer software](https://bloomerang.co/blog/volunteer-scheduling/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all). A returning volunteer shouldn’t have to refill the same information every time.

Remember, convenience often results in commitment. A well-organized system not only attracts more people but also communicates professionalism and respect for volunteers’ time.

**Pro Tip:** Sending a welcome email or packet upon sign-up expresses appreciation and strengthens the new volunteer’s connection to the organization.

## **4) Highlight the Benefits of Volunteering**

While marketing typically focuses on the cause, it’s equally important to address what volunteering offers to potential recruits. People volunteer for many reasons, including to develop skills, build social connections, or simply feel good knowing they’ve helped others. Showing potential volunteers what’s in it for them while staying authentic and mission-focused can make strong volunteer marketing even more compelling.

Nonprofits should emphasize how volunteering connects individuals to their community, helps them grow professionally, or boosts mental well-being. For example, presenting a statistic like “80% of our volunteers report feeling more connected to their community” underscores tangible benefits that resonate with potential applicants. Statistics like this can be [sourced through regular surveys](https://bloomerang.co/blog/volunteer-engagement-strategies-for-smart-organizations/?utm_source=nonprofit_tech_for_good&utm_medium=sponsored_content&utm_campaign=25e2_nb_all) to your volunteer base.

Sharing testimonials is particularly impactful. When existing volunteers speak about the positive changes they’ve experienced, prospective volunteers can envision themselves experiencing similar rewards.

**Pro Tip:** Nonprofits can motivate participation by incorporating creative incentives. Exclusive event access, public recognition, and thank-you gifts are simple yet effective ways to express gratitude while encouraging participation.

## **5) Help Current Volunteers Spread the Word**

Fostering a sense of belonging among volunteers is essential for retention, but it can also serve as a powerful recruitment tool. Volunteers who feel valued and connected to a cause are often eager to share their positive experiences with others, making them natural ambassadors for a nonprofit. To amplify this effect, nonprofits can create marketing materials specifically designed for current volunteers to share and recruit new participants.

Empowering volunteers to spread the word starts with providing the right tools. Social media templates, for example, can make it easy for volunteers to post about their experiences in a visually appealing and consistent way. These templates can include compelling images, the organization’s logo, and ready-to-use text that highlights the value of volunteering. A customizable message such as, “I volunteer with [Organization Name] because [Personal Reason]. Join me and make a difference!” offers a personal touch and inspires others to get involved.

Testimonials are another effective resource. Nonprofits can work with volunteers to create brief written or video testimonials that tell authentic, engaging stories. When shared on social media or included in newsletters, these stories can showcase the impact of volunteering from a relatable, first-hand perspective. People are more likely to act when they see the meaningful experiences of others, especially when those individuals are part of their community or network.

**Pro Tip:** Platforms like [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) simplify the process of creating customizable templates, making it easy for nonprofits to provide professional and attractive materials to current volunteers.

## **Closing the Loop**

Volunteers aren’t just helpers—they’re changemakers. They power missions, elevate causes, and make the impossible a reality. To recruit individuals who share your passion, your marketing efforts must be as intentional as the work you’re asking them to do. By crafting compelling stories, meeting potential recruits where they are, and creating an experience that feels valuable and rewarding, you’ll inspire long-term engagement and help your organization thrive.
