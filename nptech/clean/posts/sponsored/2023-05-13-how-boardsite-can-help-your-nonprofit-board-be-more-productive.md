## **5 Productivity Challenges for Board Members**

Nonprofit boards face a variety of challenges that impact their overall productivity. When your board can’t work to the best of their ability, it holds your entire organization back from reaching its true potential. Acknowledging these problems is the first step to improving your board’s operations and processes. Here are some of the biggest productivity challenges that board members face.

**1) Difficulty coordinating schedules**

One of the biggest challenges for any board is managing the directors’ busy schedules. Many of today’s nonprofits have remote or hybrid work setups, which means that directors are likely to be working in different time zones or locations. Flexible work schedules have also become the norm over the past few years, so even if your directors are in the same time zone, they may not work the same hours.

Additionally, many board directors are successful professionals with very busy schedules. They are likely to be juggling a variety of professional obligations in addition to family life and other interests outside of work.

These factors make it very difficult to schedule board meetings, which are necessary for productivity. It becomes particularly difficult when you have a large board and are trying to coordinate dozens of schedules that are constantly shifting.

**2) Onboarding**

The process of onboarding new directors can also hold your board back from being truly productive. It takes time to get new members up to speed on your organization’s goals, challenges, and communication styles, especially if your operations are disorganized.

The onboarding process often requires group or one-on-one meetings, and new members typically need to read a variety of written material as well. These steps are necessary to ensure that your entire team is on the same page, but if they’re not done efficiently, it can hold your board back from being truly productive.

**3) Engagement**

Your directors’ busy schedules don’t just limit your ability to schedule meetings. Busy schedules also make it difficult for your directors to stay engaged with key tasks between meetings. This is particularly difficult if you’re communicating entirely via email, as they’ll have to juggle several different threads just to keep up. Any sort of confusion among your board members limits their ability to get key tasks done and can put you behind schedule.

**4) Compliance**

Nonprofit boards have to adhere to comply with a variety of regulations from federal and state governments. Compliance is extremely important for your board’s reputation and success, especially when it comes to your finances.

However, compliance and government regulations can also be very confusing, especially if you don’t have support from teams within your organization who are better equipped to handle it. It’s easy to get bogged down managing compliance issues and keeping records organized rather than moving forward.

**5) Lack of agenda and planning**

Because of the busy schedules that board members have, it can be difficult to adequately plan meetings before they happen. When you don’t have time to [make a meeting agenda beforehand](https://boardsite.app/features/agenda-minutes-builder/), the conversation can quickly get sidetracked and devolve into casual or irrelevant conversation. Without an agenda, it’s also very easy for one or two people to dominate the meeting, which means that not everyone will get a chance to share their ideas. An agenda ensures that the meeting stays on track and that all key topics get covered.
