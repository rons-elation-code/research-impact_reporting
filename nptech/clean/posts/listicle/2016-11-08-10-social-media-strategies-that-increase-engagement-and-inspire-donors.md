## **1. Embrace social media fundraising.**

- 59% of people of who **[engage with causes](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633167228555/)** on social media donate money to nonprofits.
- 27% of donors worldwide **[cite social media](http://techreport.ngo/2016.html)** as the communication tool that inspires them the most often to give.
- **[Online giving](http://mrbenchmarks.com/)** is set for **[exponential](http://www.networkforgood.com/digitalgivingindex/) [growth](http://www.networkforgood.com/digitalgivingindex/)** over the next decade **([especially through social networks](https://www.facebook.com/nrdc.org/))**.
- Invest resources in social media fundraising now – or regret it later!

## 2. Be an early adopter of digital payments.

- Sign up for **[Facebook’s new donation](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2016/07/10/what-your-nonprofit-needs-to-know-about-facebooks-new-fundraising-tools/) [tools](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2016/07/10/what-your-nonprofit-needs-to-know-about-facebooks-new-fundraising-tools/)**. You must be a 501(c)(3) and have a verified page.
- Twitter has launched the ability to donate to political campaigns and is **[testing nonprofit donations](https://twitter.com/Water/status/792350306134700032)**. A good first step would be creating your **[$](https://cash.me/cashtags)[Cashtag](https://cash.me/cashtags)**.
- Sign up for **[Google for Nonprofits](https://www.google.com/nonprofits/)** to accept donations through **[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6591KnPeh-c)** and possibly Google+.
- There’s also **[Apple](http://www.apple.com/apple-pay/) [Pay](http://www.apple.com/apple-pay/)**, **[Android Pay](https://www.android.com/pay/)**, and **[Snapcash](https://support.snapchat.com/en-US/ca/snapcash)**.

## **3. Craft a content strategy.**

- To inspire social media followers to donate, you must **[embrace content marketing](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633169865007/)**.
- Begin by **[writing a social media strategy](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2016/02/13/how-to-write-a-social-media-fundraising-strategy-for-your-nonprofit/)**: 1) Conduct an online technology audit. 2) Name goals and action items. 3) Create and fund a budget.
- Next, craft a content strategy i.e., decide what types of content your nonprofit will create and how often and **[create an editorial calendar](http://www.nptechforgood.com/editoral-calendar-2016/).**

## 4. Write good stories.

- Best Friends Animal Society: **[Breaking free from a dog anxiety order](http://bestfriends.org/stories-blog-videos/latest-news/breaking-free-dog-anxiety-disorder) ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/bestfriends/status/704352067737923584), [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/bestfriendsanimalsociety/posts/10153876414016425))**
- One Campaign: **[10 things girls and women could be doing instead of collecting water](http://www.one.org/us/2016/02/26/10-things-girls-and-women-could-be-doing-instead-of-collecting-water/) ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/ONECampaign/status/704047236985057280), [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/ONE/posts/10153524002049472))**
- Ocean Conservancy: **[The Ocean’s Least Wanted: 4 Invasive Species to Know](http://blog.oceanconservancy.org/2016/02/24/the-oceans-least-wanted-4-invasive-species-to-know/) ([Twitter](https://twitter.com/OurOcean/status/702951284165492736), [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/oceanconservancy/posts/10154570976599027), [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/oceanconservancy/))**

## 5. Create visual content.

- On Facebook, **[visually appealing photos and compelling graphics](https://www.facebook.com/FeedingAmerica/photos/pb.23875187730.-2207520000.1456841827./10153912779622731/?type=1&theater)** receive up to 4X more engagement (shares and likes).
- **[Tweets with images](https://twitter.com/AusConservation/status/704442268975919104)** receive 18% more clicks and 3X more engagement (retweets and likes).
- Create **[inspirational quotes](https://www.instagram.com/p/BB-d4aYLOQI/?taken-by=glblctzn), [powerful stats](https://twitter.com/FeedingAmerica/status/700378739373375489), [infographics](http://techreport.ngo/2016.html), [title images](https://twitter.com/nonprofitorgs/status/704289565738188800), [event invites](https://twitter.com/Heifer/status/634534149357993984),** and **[calls-to-action](https://twitter.com/Survival/status/618505348517789696).**

## 6. Know the science of social media.

- **Facebook:** Post 5-7 times weekly (at least once on the weekend) and then monitor your **[Insights](https://www.facebook.com/nonprofitorgs/insights/)**. Post visually appealing photos and graphics and now that the new Reactions are live, test positive and **[negative content](https://www.facebook.com/AfricanWildlifeFoundation/photos/a.451901523948.240322.11918108948/10154106333573949/?type=3&theater)** to see which inspires more engagement. Limit post text to **[80\* characters or less](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633167355915/)**.
- **Twitter:** Tweet or retweet every **[one or two hours](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633164818081/)**. Use **[Buffer](https://bufferapp.com/)** to schedule tweets in the evenings and on the weekends. Visually compelling photos or graphics receive 3X the engagement as text tweets – especially if the images are **[properly sized for Twitter Mobile (800 x 420 pixels)](https://twitter.com/nonprofitorgs/media)**.
- **Instagram:** **[Post or](https://instagram.com/nonprofitorgs/) [regram](https://instagram.com/nonprofitorgs/)** once per day alternating between morning and late afternoon. Nonprofits that consistently use **[hashtags](https://www.instagram.com/p/BCOa54rQun6/)** have up to 3X more followers.

## 7. Tap into the power of hashtag fundraising.

- **[#](https://www.givingtuesday.org/)[GivingTuesday](https://www.givingtuesday.org/) [2015](https://www.givingtuesday.org/)** was very successful and the trend will likely continue in 2016 – not only on #GivingTuesday, but also on **[cause awareness days](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2015/12/30/2016-cause-awareness-giving-day-calendar/)**. Begin promoting your hashtag fundraising campaign one month before in email and on social media: 1) Create a **[microsite](http://enset.ngo/)** or **[web page](http://www.wateraid.org/uk/get-involved/blue4water)** with the date, hashtag, and three clear calls-to-action. 2) Create a series of **[social media promotion graphics](https://twitter.com/UNDPGeneva/status/702085948989898752)**. 3) After the hashtag fundraising campaign is over, **[write a summary of success](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/12/16/how-to-tap-into-the-power-of-cause-awareness-days/)** and tell donors and supporters to “Save the Date” for next year.

## 8. Broadcast your story live.

- **Facebook Live:** On desktop or on your mobile device, your nonprofit can **[go Live on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/facebookmedia/best-practices/live)**.
- **Instagram****:** When the impulse strikes, post **[photos](https://www.instagram.com/p/BB5Y4rTx92S/?taken-by=girlsinc)** and **[videos](https://www.instagram.com/p/BBQogNctvri/?taken-by=aspca)** in real-time. Create **[photo and video essays](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2016/10/22/a-day-trip-the-hague-netherlands-international-city-of-peace-and-justice/)**.
- **Periscope:** Connected to Twitter, **[Periscope](https://www.periscope.tv/nonprofitorgs)** is a mobile app that allows you to broadcast live. **[Broadcasts can be recorded](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2015/11/13/how-to-report-live-from-nonprofit-events-using-periscope/)**, saved to your Camera Roll, and then uploaded to YouTube, Facebook, etc.

## 9. Grow your followers.

- Build your email list and then use your **[email newsletter](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633169527308/)** to grow your social media followers.
- Convert your donors into social media followers during the online donation process. Ensure that you have a call-to-follow on your **[“Thank You” landing page](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/07/06/6-fresh-ideas-for-your-nonprofits-thank-you-landing-page/)** and in your **[“Thank You” email](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/203154633169867266/)**.
- Prominently feature your email newsletter opt-in and social media calls-to-follow on your **[website](http://www.sentencingproject.org/), [blog](http://www.nptechforgood.com/)**, and in print materials.

## 10. Invest in social media staff.

- Your organization’s ability to embrace social media as a storytelling and fundraising tools depends upon the **[hours](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2015/02/21/how-much-time-social-media-requires-for-fundraising-success/)** and **[salary](http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Social_Media_Manager/Salary)** that you are willing to invest.
- That said, for your social media staff to be effective on social media, they need an **[advanced skill set](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/07/14/10-must-have-skills-for-nonprofit-new-media-managers/)**. If they don’t have the skills necessary, then provide the training and time necessary for them to learn – or hire new staff.
- *Social media is a waste of time and resources if it is not done well.*
