# 1) Brackets for Good [bfg.org](https://bfg.org/)

*Brackets for Good* hosts online, bracket-style fundraising tournaments that encourage donors to out-fundraise their opponents on behalf of their favorite nonprofit. The winner of the tournament receives a $100,000 grand prize.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Branckets-for-Good.jpg?resize=600%2C435)](https://bfg.org/)

# 2) Bstow [bstow.com](https://bstow.com/)

*Bstow* is a mobile app that allows users to easily roundup credit and debit purchases to be automatically donated to their favorite nonprofits. Users can set a monthly limit and donate to any nonprofit in the United States.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/bstow.jpg?resize=600%2C365)](https://bstow.com/index.html)

# 3) Dipjar [dipjar.com](https://www.dipjar.com/)

*DipJar* enables cashless donations via tip jars and donation boxes for credit cards. The service manages every aspect of transactions, from processing to payout.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/Dipjar-1.png?resize=600%2C280)](https://www.dipjar.com/)

# 4) Goodpin [good.pn](https://www.good.pn "http://www.good.pn")

*Goodpin (currently in beta)* is a hub of special offers on products and services offered by brands that can be redeemed by making a donation to participating nonprofits.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Goodpin.png?resize=600%2C273)](https://www.good.pn)

# 5) Goodworld [goodworld.me](https://goodworld.me)

*Goodworld* enables donors to give on Facebook and Twitter with two taps and a #donate hashtag. Recently relaunched with an improved design and donation process, the service now also offers **[two-tap giving on the web](https://goodworld.me/60/beagle-freedom-project)**.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Goodworld.png?resize=600%2C276)](https://goodworld.me)

# 6) Internet of Things (IoT) Buttons [amazon.com/iotbutton](https://aws.amazon.com/iotbutton/)

*The AWS IoT Button* is a programmable button based on the **[Amazon Dash Button](https://www.amazon.com/Dash-Buttons/b?ie=UTF8&node=10667898011)** hardware. In addition to these **[26 interesting hacks of the AWS Iot button](http://www.dashbuttondudes.com/blog/2015/12/11/26-amazon-dash-button-hacks)**, one coder has created an [**Amazon Dash button that donates to the ACLU**](http://gizmodo.com/how-to-make-an-amazon-dash-button-that-gives-the-aclu-1792091727). In 2017 it will be interesting to watch if the nonprofit sector adopts the technology and creates more IoT Buttons for donating and advocacy. That said, there is one problem with the Amazon Dash Button: t*he battery is not replaceable or rechargeable*, so the hardware does need to be more sustainable for it to be widely adopted by the social good sector. Perhaps a new button hardware will be introduced by a service other than Amazon?

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/iot-button.png?resize=600%2C272)](https://aws.amazon.com/iotbutton/)

# 7) Spotfund [spotfund.com](http://spotfund.com/)

*Spotfund* is a location-based mobile app that allows users to search for stories (created by nonprofits) to donate to that are in near proximity or located in their local community.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Spotfund.png?resize=600%2C287)](http://www.spotfund.com/)

# 8) Tiltify [tiltify.com](https://tiltify.com)

*Tiltify* is a crowdfunding service for gamers that enables them to raise money through online streams like **[Twitch.tv](https://www.twitch.tv/)** and YouTube. Gamers can use the service to recruit online donations, create rewards, track progress, and create fundraising teams.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Tilitify.png?resize=600%2C349)](https://tiltify.com)
