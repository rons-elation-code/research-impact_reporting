We’re also getting close to a new generation of mobile fundraising apps that are sustainable and have long term appeal to donors (*think:* giving portfolio, gamification, and one-tap, finger scan giving). That said, fundraising tools come and go and long-term financial sustainability is tough for social good entrepreneurs to achieve (*muchos kudos* for trying), but the fundraising tools list for 2016 (**[view the 2015 list](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2015/04/11/9-fundraising-tools-to-watch-in-2015/)**) represent eight ideas whose time have come in the nonprofit sector.

---

## 1. Apple Pay [apple.com/apple-pay](http://www.apple.com/apple-pay/)

In addition to paying for goods and services in stores, Apply Pay enables iPhone and iPad users to make payments with a tap and a finger scan. The service will likely be adopted by many fundraising service providers in 2016 with **[Crowdster for Nonprofits](http://crowdster.com/)** being one of the first.

[![Apple Pay](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Apple-Pay.png?resize=600%2C407)](http://www.apple.com/apple-pay/)

## 2. Facebook Fundraisers and Donate Buttons [donations.fb.com](https://donations.fb.com/)

“Fundraisers” are pages where *verified and approved nonprofits* can raise money for specific fundraising campaigns and “Donate” buttons enable nonprofits to accept donations inside of Facebook directly from their Page and posts.

[![facebook fundraisers and donate now buttons](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/facebook-fundraisers-and-donate-now-buttons.jpg?resize=600%2C268)](https://donations.fb.com/)

## 3. Give A Dime [giveadime.org](https://giveadime.org/)

A service that rounds up your credit or debit card purchases to the nearest dollar and then donates the funds raised to your chosen charity.

[![Give A Dime 2](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Give-A-Dime-2.png?resize=600%2C284)](https://giveadime.org/)

## 4. Givelocity [givelocity.com](https://www.givelocity.com/)

A platform that enables individuals to create groups where members donate $1 (minimum donation) and vote each month on which nonprofit will receive the lump sum.

[![Givelocity](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Givelocity.png?resize=600%2C329)](https://www.givelocity.com/)

## 5. Givolio [givolio.com](http://givolio.com/)

A mobile app that enables donors to donate to their favorite nonprofit in just a few taps. Donors can build a giving portfolio, view their giving history, and receive tax reports via email.

[![Givolio](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Givolio.jpg?resize=600%2C289)](http://givolio.com/)

## 6. Omaze [omaze.com](https://www.omaze.com/)

A website that encourages individuals to donate in order to win once-in-a-lifetime experiences.

[![Omaze](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Omaze.png?resize=600%2C270)](https://www.omaze.com/)

## 7. Pledgeling [pledgeling.com](https://www.pledgeling.com/enterprise/)

A service that allows businesses to easily set up an employee giving program.

[![Pledgeling](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Pledgeling.png?resize=600%2C290)](https://www.pledgeling.com/enterprise/)

## 8. YouGiveGoods [yougivegoods.com](https://yougivegoods.com)

A platform that enables individuals, businesses, and nonprofits to create online food and supply drives.

[![YouGiveGoods](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/YouGiveGoods.png?resize=600%2C338)](https://yougivegoods.com/)
