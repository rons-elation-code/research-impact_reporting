According to the **[2020 Blackbaud Charitable Giving Report](https://institute.blackbaud.com/charitable-giving-report/)**, online giving grew 20.7% in 2020 (!!) and accounts for 13% of a nonprofit’s total revenue – and those numbers will continue to grow. Also important to be aware of is that 28% of online donations made in 2020 occurred on a mobile device. Having a [**mobile-compatible website is now a standard best practice**](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2020/01/05/10-website-design-best-practices-for-nonprofits/) and that is especially true for your nonprofit’s donation pages.

# 1) Ensure that your donation page(s) have the same branding as your website.

Sending donors a third-party payment site, such as PayPal, is problematic if that is the only way that donors can give. According to [**Network for Good**](https://www.networkforgood.com/), one in six online donors will drop out of the donation process if they are sent to a third-party website. A good example of a donation page with proper branding is the [**World Wildlife Fund’s primary donation page**](https://support.worldwildlife.org/site/SPageServer?pagename=main_onetime):

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/WWF-Donation-Pages.jpg?resize=847%2C708)](https://support.worldwildlife.org/site/SPageServer?pagename=main_onetime)

Accepting PayPal in addition to credit cards is a good idea to accommodate those donors that prefer to give through PayPal, but don’t make all donors use PayPal. It’s also worth noting that according to the [**Global Trends in Giving Report**](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report/past-reports), 10% of donors worldwide prefer to give directly through their bank account. The World Wildlife Fund accepts all three types of payments – credits cards, bank account (ACH), and PayPal:

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/WWF.jpg?resize=773%2C503)](https://support.worldwildlife.org/site/SPageServer?pagename=main_onetime)

# 2) Add a monthly giving option to your primary donation page and create an additional donation page where monthly giving is the only option.

Monthly donors give $35.46 per month – or $425 a year, according to the [**Blackbaud Luminate Online Benchmarks Report**](https://hello.blackbaud.com/rs/053-MXJ-131/images/2018_Benchmark_Report_FINAL.pdf). As a result, securing more monthly donor revenue is a high priority for nonprofits. Most donation pages include a checkbox for the donor to opt-in to upgrade their donation to monthly which is a standard best practice. Some nonprofits even have monthly giving set as the default on their primary donation page which can increase your nonprofit’s number of monthly gifts by as much as 5.5% (also according to Blackbaud). As an example, see the primary donation page for the [**Humane Society of the United States**](https://secure.humanesociety.org/site/Donation2;jsessionid=00000000.app30120a?idb=972341874&df_id=24512&mfc_pref=T&24512.donation=form1&NONCE_TOKEN=AF23906EEAB8867A681CE02AF95FE5C9&s_src=web_topnav_donate&24512_donation=form1):

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/HSUS.jpg?resize=960%2C617)](https://secure.humanesociety.org/site/Donation2;jsessionid=00000000.app30120a?idb=972341874&df_id=24512&mfc_pref=T&24512.donation=form1&NONCE_TOKEN=AF23906EEAB8867A681CE02AF95FE5C9&s_src=web_topnav_donate&24512_donation=form1)

# 3) Add a monthly giving pop-up to your primary donation page.

According to [**NextAfter**](https://recurringgiving.com/), only 14% of organizations prompt one-time donors to upgrade to a monthly gift (also known as a recurring gift) during the donation process. That’s a missed opportunity because NextAfter also found that a “Give Monthly” pop-up results in a 64% increase in monthly donations.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Yes.jpg?resize=600%2C283)](https://recurringgiving.com/)

# **4) Experiment with default donation amounts.**

Surprisingly, there is little concrete data on which set of donation amounts results in the highest increase in online giving. That’s likely because every nonprofit and their donors are different. As a best practice, study and mimic large nonprofits similar to yours in mission and programs with years of experience.

For example, the [**Natural Resources Defense Council has $35, $50, $75, $100 and $200 donation amounts**](https://act.nrdc.org/donate/one-time?&locsrc=nr-glb-nav-btn&source=WEBMEM) listed with $50 set as the default donation amount. NRDC is highlighting a $50 default donation with a $35 donation as the least amount to give which presents a psychological benchmark to potential donors: *$35 is a good minimal donation, but $50 is better and only $15 more*. Donors that want to give more than $50 will select a higher donation amount. That said, It’s also a best practice to include an empty field where donors can enter any donation amount.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/NRDC.jpg?resize=960%2C632)](https://act.nrdc.org/donate/one-time?&locsrc=nr-glb-nav-btn&source=WEBMEM)

The [**Rainforest Alliance**](https://secure.rainforest-alliance.org/site/Donation2;jsessionid=00000000.app30131a?idb=604770889&df_id=7202&7202.donation=form1&mfc_pref=T&NONCE_TOKEN=B91DECD212C7AEEC3B0CB6A15A8D4C18&_ga=2.219184345.1915760770.1586371442-1513310914.1586371442&7202_donation=form1) takes it a step further with $75 set as the default and a message of “Most people are giving $75 right now. Please give what you can.”

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Rainforest-Alliance.jpg?resize=960%2C517)](https://act.350.org/donate/build/)

In contrast, [**350.org**](https://act.350.org/donate/build/) has a $35 default donation amount with a $10 option as the lowest amount. Smaller amounts are likely going to become more common as our economy adjusts to the COVID-19 crisis.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/350.jpg?resize=960%2C710)](https://act.350.org/donate/build/)

Knowing the ideal donation default amount for your nonprofit will require that you analyze your donor data to determine your average online donation and then experiment with default amounts to learn which default donation amount results in more income. To begin, add $10 to your average online donation and set that as your default donation amount.

Finally, what all three organizations listed above have in common is that they use large buttons for donation amounts rather than radio buttons. NextAfter has also found that using big rectangle buttons rather than small radio buttons results in a [**22.9% increase in donations**](https://www.nextafter.com/blog/general-donation-page-ideas/).

# **5) Add an optional mobile number field.**

According to [**MobileCause**](https://secure.pancan.org/site/Donation2?df_id=2681&mfc_pref=T&2681.donation=form1), 90% of text messages are read within three minutes of being sent while only 24% of emails are being read at all. Nonprofits are missing a big opportunity to stay more connected with their donors by not capturing their mobile phone numbers. The **[Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)](https://nonprofitfederation.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/TCPA-Factsheet.pdf)** asserts that if you have a donor’s phone number, or a donor supplies it, it is implied permission to text them any future messages. You can text message donors information about fundraising campaigns, events, emergencies, and volunteer opportunities – just to name a few.

# **6) Add charity rating badges and program vs. operating expense graphics.**

If your nonprofit has been reviewed by [**Charity Navigator**](https://www.charitynavigator.org/), the [**BBB Wise Giving Alliance**](http://give.org/), [**Charity Watch**](https://www.charitywatch.org/), or **[GiveWell](https://www.givewell.org/)** and your reviews are good, then add your rating badges to your donation page(s). If your nonprofit is too small or does not meet the qualifications to be reviewed by Charity Navigator, the BBB Wise Giving Alliance, Charity Watch, or GiveWell, then create an account on [**GreatNonprofits**](http://greatnonprofits.org) and ask for your donors to rate and review your organization. You also want to regularly monitor your ratings and reviews on [**GuideStar**](https://www.guidestar.org/). Once your nonprofit has built up your user-generated ratings and reviews on GreatNonprofits and GuideStar, then add those badges to your donation pages as well. For example, [**Children International**](https://www.children.org/make-a-difference/donate#give-today):

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Children-International.png?resize=900%2C579)](https://www.children.org/make-a-difference/donate#give-today)

Also, provided your program vs. operating expense ratio is good ([**75%/25%**](https://www.today.com/money/you-give-check-out-charitys-ratings-1D80330057)), create a simple pie chart graphic that illuminates your program and operating costs. That said, there is a growing movement against low overhead as the primary indicator of a nonprofit’s effectiveness. [**Overhead Myth advocates**](https://www.qgiv.com/blog/the-overhead-myth/) assert that nonprofits cannot be successful in achieving their programs if they cannot invest in highly-qualified staff and technology.

# **7) Add giving impact statements.**

Donors respond well to statements that clarify the impact of their donations. For example, a $50 donation provides a spay/neuter surgery or a $10 donation provides five meals at a homeless shelter. Adding impact statements helps donors conceptualize how they are making a difference and can increase their donation amount. For example, [**BuildOn**](https://www.buildon.org/take-action/donate-now/):

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/BuildOn.jpg?resize=960%2C763)](https://www.buildon.org/take-action/donate-now/)

# **8)** Add a tribute giving option to your primary donation page and create an additional donation page where tribute giving is the only option.

According to the [**Global Trends in Giving Report**](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report/past-reports), 33% of donors worldwide prefer to give tribute gifts (26% for Millennials, 31% for Gen Xers, and 41% for Baby Boomers). Also known as honor and/or memorial gifts, most nonprofits add the option to their primary donation page, such as the [**Special Olympics**](https://give.specialolympics.org/page/contribute/donatenow):[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Special-Olympics.jpg?resize=960%2C600)](https://give.specialolympics.org/page/contribute/donatenow)

However, to effectively promote tribute gifts during the holidays or for special occasions in print, in email, and on social media, your nonprofit should also create an [**additional donation page just for tribute giving**](https://give.specialolympics.org/page/contribute/ISdonate-honor):

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Special-Olympics-2.jpg?resize=960%2C569)](https://give.specialolympics.org/page/contribute/ISdonate-honor)

It’s worth noting that also according to the [**Global Trends in Giving Report**](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report/past-reports), the most popular tribute gifts with donors are memorial gifts (43%), birthdays (25%), and religious holidays (10%).

# 9) Create a compelling, action-oriented “Thank You for Your Donation” page.

A “Thank You for Your Donation” landing page is the page that donors land on after their donation has been processed. It’s an important page because it is one of the few times you have the hyper-focused attention of your donors as they wait for their payments to be confirmed. Yet surprisingly it is one of the most overlooked pages on a nonprofit’s website. In addition to thanking your donors, use the page to convert your donors into social media followers and to inspire them to get more involved.

![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/WWF-3.png?resize=600%2C510)

# **10) Include a phone number and mailing address.**

For those donors that prefer to give offline, they may visit your nonprofit’s website and click the “Donate” button only become frustrated that the primary donation page does not include information on how to give via mail or over the phone. [**Environmental Defense provides that information (and other ways to give) in the sidebar on their primary donation page**](https://membership.onlineaction.org/site/Donation2?df_id=19180&19180.donation=form1&addl_info=nav-button&conversion_pg=www.edf.org%2F&_ga=2.27739967.2044423247.1586891645-903284160.1586445063):
