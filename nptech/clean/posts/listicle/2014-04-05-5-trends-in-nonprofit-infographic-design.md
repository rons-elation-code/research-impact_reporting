# 5 Trends in Nonprofit Infographic Design

> As the fiscal year comes to a close and nonprofits are currently crafting next year’s budget, adding a line item for infographic design would be a very wise investment. From print fundraising appeals to digital annuals reports to compelling storytelling through tweets and status updates, the social science of online behavior confirms again and again that messages are most effectively communicated through images. Custom infographic design fees vary widely depending on complexity (ranging from $1-10,000), but a creative, Adobe Photoshop-skilled new media manager can customize stock infographics or no matter your design experience, utilize services like Piktochart, Infogram, and easelly. From print fundraising appeals to digital annuals reports to compelling storytelling through tweets and status updates, the social science of online behavior confirms again and again that messages are most effectively communicated through images. Custom infographic design fees vary widely depending on complexity (ranging from $1-10,000), but a creative, Adobe Photoshop-skilled new media manager can customize [stock infographics](http://www.shutterstock.com/cat.mhtml?searchterm=infographic&search_group=&lang=en&language=en&search_source=search_form&version=llv1) or no matter your design experience, utilize services like [Piktochart](http://piktochart.com/), [Infogram](http://infogr.am/), and [easelly](http://www.easel.ly/). That said, the technology of infographics are evolving quickly. They can now be designed in video format, animated, and [responsively designed](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/02/25/11-responsively-designed-nonprofit-websites-to-study-and-learn-from/) to ensure mobile compatibility.
