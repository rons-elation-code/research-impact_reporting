## 1. Adding a complete address to your Facebook Page activates Facebook Location.

Facebook Location enables fans to check-in to your page and review your nonprofit.

[![Add Adresss to Facebook Page](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Add-Adresss-to-Facebook-Page.png?resize=600%2C238)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Add-Adresss-to-Facebook-Page.png)

[![Facebook Reviews and Check-ins](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Facebook-Reviews-and-Check-ins.png?resize=600%2C237)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Facebook-Reviews-and-Check-ins.png)

## 2. Fans can “Save” your nonprofit’s Facebook Page.

Nonprofits that have added Facebook Location activate the ability for fans to save their page as a place.

[![Facebook Saved Pages](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Facebook-Saved-Pages.png?resize=600%2C143)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Facebook-Saved-Pages.png)

[![Saved Places](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Saved-Places.png?resize=600%2C526)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Saved-Places.png)

## 3. A period is the difference between a public mention and a reply on Twitter.

When you use a Twitter username as the first word in your tweet, you must add a period before the username for all your followers to see your tweet.

[![Period before a username on Twitter](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Period-before-a-username-on-Twitter.png?resize=600%2C276)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Period-before-a-username-on-Twitter.png)

## 4. The optimal aspect ratio for Twitter photos is 2:1.

For your followers to see a full preview of the photos you upload to Twitter, photos should be sized with a 2:1 aspect ratio. For example, 1024 x 512 pixels.

[![Twitter photo size](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Twitter-photo-size.png?resize=600%2C332)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Twitter-photo-size.png)

## 5. Tweets between 100 and 115 characters are more likely to be retweeted.

If your nonprofit is not getting retweeted, then you’re not doing right. [Know the science of Twitter retweets](http://danzarrella.com/).

[![length_retweets](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/length_retweets.jpg?resize=600%2C410)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/length_retweets.jpg)

## 6. Admins can edit posts on Google+.

Nonprofit can easily correct spelling and grammar mistakes on Google+ posts.

[![Edit Google+ Posts](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Edit-Google%2B-Posts.png?resize=600%2C404)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Edit-Google%2B-Posts.png)

Nonprofits can use Pinterest Place Pins to [create a board that showcases location-based campaigns](http://www.pinterest.com/visitbritain/unesco-world-heritage-sites-in-the-uk/).
