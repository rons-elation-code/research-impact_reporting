Our Certificate in Social Media Marketing & Fundraising covers the fundamentals of launching and maintaining a social media marketing and fundraising strategy for your nonprofit. Participants will learn how to use Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter to engage and inspire supporters and donors.

Based on [*101 Digital Marketing & Fundraising Best Practices for Nonprofits*](http://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/), the certificate program requires the completion of four webinars. The webinars only cost $20-30 each so that nonprofit professionals worldwide can earn the certificate without having to make a significant financial investment.

**The required four webinars are:**

**[$20] July 13: [How to Create a Social Media Marketing Strategy](http://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/webinar-how-to-create-a-social-media-strategy-for-your-nonprofit/)  
[$30] July 27: [Facebook & Instagram Best Practices for Nonprofits](http://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/webinar-facebook-instagram-best-practices-for-nonprofits/)  
[$30] August 10: [LinkedIn & Twitter Best Practices for Nonprofits](http://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/webinar-linkedin-twitter-best-practices-for-nonprofits/)  
[$20] August 24: [Emerging Social Media Trends for Nonprofits](http://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/webinar-emerging-social-media-trends-for-nonprofits/)**

To earn the certificate, you can attend the webinars live or view the recordings – or a combination of both. A link to the recording and a comprehensive set of notes are emailed to registrants 24-48 hours after each webinar is over. Please note that the live webinars for the certificate will not be presented again until January 2023.

**To claim your certificate:**

1. Attend/view all four webinars
2. Save your receipts and the secret passphrases presented in each webinar
3. Request your certificate via email at the end of the fourth webinar from [**Heather Mansfield**](http://www.nptechforgood.com/heather-mansfield/), founder and editor-in-chief of Nonprofit Tech for Good
