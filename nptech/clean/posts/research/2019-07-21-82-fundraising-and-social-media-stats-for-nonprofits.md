## Fundraising Stats

- The average online gift in 2018 was $96.40 USD ([**Blackbaud Luminate Online Report**](https://hi.blackbaud.com/luminate/)).
- 8.5% of overall fundraising in 2018 came from online giving ([**Charitable Giving Report**](https://institute.blackbaud.com/asset/2018-charitable-giving-report/)).
- 54% of donors worldwide prefer to give via credit or debit card. 11% via direct mail/post. 10% via bank/wire transfer. 9% via PayPal. 4% via mobile app/wallet. 1% via text message (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).
- The number of new donors giving to charity declined by 7.3% in 2018. The number of new retained donors dropped 14.9% ([**Fundraising Effectiveness Project**](http://afpfep.org/)).
- The typical return donor made a second donation around 349 days after their first donation. However, 19% of donors return within the first 90 days ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).
- 29% of annual donation volume occurs from #GivingTuesday through the end of the year ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).
- 53% of nonprofit staff who are extremely or somewhat satisfied in their current roles donate to the organization where they work ([**World-Changing Work Report**](https://go.classy.org/nonprofit-professionals-report-2019)).
- In 2018, Americans gave $427.7 billion USD to charity. 77% of the total was made by individuals, 18% by foundations, and 5% by corporations (**[Giving USA Report](https://givingusa.org/)**).
- Individual giving Decreased from 70% in 2017 to 68% in 2018. Religious organizations (-3.9%) and educational organizations (-3.7%) experienced the largest drop in giving (**[Giving USA Report](https://givingusa.org/)**).
- 92% of donors say it is important that NGOs, nonprofits, and charities make a concerted effort to protect their contact and financial information from data breaches (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).

### International Giving

- 31% of donors worldwide giving to NGOs, nonprofits, and charities located outside of their country of residence (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).
- Donating money has increased in 2017 among those donors from developed nations (from 40% in 2016 to 42%), but has declined among developing countries (from 25% in 2016 to 24%)
- The top countries where individuals give the most often (not necessarily the most money) are (1) Myanmar, (2) The United States, (3) New Zealand, (4) Australia, and ; (5) Ireland ([**Charities Aid Foundation World Giving Index**](https://www.cafonline.org/)).

### Email Fundraising

- Email messaging drives 13% of all online revenue ([**M+R Benchmarks Report**](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)).
- For every 1,000 fundraising messages delivered via email, nonprofits raise $45 USD ([**M+R Benchmarks Report**](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)).
- Nonprofits sent an average of 59 email messages per subscriber in 2018 ([**M+R Benchmarks Report**](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)).
- Nonprofits lose 20.18% of their email revenue ($92.8 million) due to spam filters ([**EveryAction**](https://www.everyaction.com/)).
- 63% of nonprofits worldwide regularly send email updates and fundraising appeals ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- 68% of online donors most trust email addresses and websites that use the .org domain (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).

### **Recurring Giving**

- Monthly giving revenue grew by an average of 18.37% in 2018 ([**Blackbaud Luminate Online Report**](https://hi.blackbaud.com/luminate/)).
- Monthly donors give $35.46 per month – or $425 a year ([**Blackbaud Luminate Online Report**](https://hi.blackbaud.com/luminate/)).
- Only 14% of organizations prompt one-time donors to upgrade to a recurring gift during the donation process (**[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/)**).
- 91% of organizations stopped acknowledging recurring gifts by month three (**[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/)**).
- A pop-up asking a one-time donor to upgrade to become a monthly donor results in a 64% increase in monthly donations (**[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/)**).
- One-time donors that upgrade to become monthly donors tend to do so around four months after their one-time donation ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).

### Mobile Giving

- 24% of online donations in 2018 were made via a mobile device ([**Charitable Giving Report**](https://institute.blackbaud.com/asset/2018-charitable-giving-report/)).
- 77% of donors who first donate from a mobile device make their second donation from a mobile device ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).
- 15% of nonprofits worldwide regularly send text messages to donors and supporters ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Small nonprofits have an average of 2,284 text message subscribers. Medium have 17,076. Large have 22,299 ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- The average text-to-donate gift is $106 (**[MobileCause](https://www.mobilecause.com/)**).
- 84% text-to-pledge gifts are fulfilled (**[MobileCause](https://www.mobilecause.com/)**).

### Tribute Gifts

- 33% of donors give tribute gifts to family and friends. Donors are most interested in giving tribute gifts as memorials (43%), birthday gifts (25%), and on religious holidays (10%) (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).

### Matching Gifts

- An estimated $2-3 billion USD is donated through matching gift programs annually, however, an additional $4-7 billion USD in matching gift funds goes unclaimed every year ([**Double the Donation**](https://doublethedonation.com/)).
- 12% of total corporate cash contributions to nonprofits are made through matching gift programs ([**Chief Executives for Corporate Purpose**](https://cecp.co/)).
- 1 in 3 donors indicate they’d give a larger gift if matching is applied to their donation (**[The Big Give Research Initiative](https://www.thebiggive.org.uk/s/)**).

### Peer-to-Peer Fundraising

- 33% of nonprofits worldwide using a peer-to-peer fundraising platform ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- 47% of peer-to-peer donors are first-time donors to an organization **[(Cathexis Partners](https://cathexispartners.com/)).**
- For every share on social media, a peer-to-peer fundraising campaign raises an average of $13USD (**[CrowdRise](https://www.crowdrise.com/)**[)](https://www.crowdrise.com/).
- One-time fundraisers raise, on average, $222 USD. $501 USD is raised by return fundraisers ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).
- 14% of peer-to-peer fundraisers became fundraisers again for the same organization ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).

### #GivingTuesday

- The average online gift on #GivingTuesday was $105 USD ([**Giving Tuesday**](https://www.givingtuesday.org/)).
- In 2018, online donors made 29% of their #GivingTuesday gifts via a mobile device ([**Charitable Giving Report**](https://institute.blackbaud.com/asset/2018-charitable-giving-report/)).
- On average, nonprofits gain 4X the number of new donors on #GivingTuesday that they do on a typical day ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).

### Giving by Gender

- 70% of giving circles are majority women. For women, giving is based on empathy for others. For men, giving is often more about self-interest (**[Women’s Philanthropy Institute](https://philanthropy.iupui.edu/institutes/womens-philanthropy-institute/index.html)**).
- 61% of first-time donors are women and 59% of return donors are also women. ([**The State of Modern Philanthropy Report**](https://go.classy.org/reports/the-state-of-modern-philanthropy-2019)).
- The top five causes for women are children and youth (15%), animals and wildlife (13%), health and wellness (10%), human and social services (8%), and hunger and homelessness (8%)  (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).
- The top five causes for men are children and youth (15%), health and wellness (11%), human and social services (9%), faith and spirituality (8%), and hunger and homelessness (8%) (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).

### **Facebook**

- For every $100 in online revenue, $1.77 is raised through Facebook Charitable Giving Tools [**(M+R Benchmarks Report)**.](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/twitter-announces-full-rollout-of-the-platforms-new-desktop-experience/558788/)
- 88% of donors who have given through Facebook Charitable Giving Tools say they are likely to do it again in the future (**[Global Trends in Giving Report](https://www.funraise.org/giving-report)**).
- 93% of NGOs worldwide have a Facebook Page. 25% post once daily on Facebook, 23% post once every other day, 19% post once weekly, 18% post twice or more daily, and 15% post less than once weekly ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Small nonprofits have an average if 8,722 Facebook Followers. Medium have 32,092. Large have 109,158 ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Facebook posts only reach an average of 4% of a nonprofit page’s fans **([M+R Benchmarks Report](https://mrbenchmarks.com/))**.
- The Engagement Score (engaged users divided by total page fans) for an average Facebook post is 0.31%. Video posts have the highest Engagement Score at 0.33% **([M+R Benchmarks Report](https://mrbenchmarks.com/))**.
- 41% of NGOs have used Facebook to report live from a special event or to showcase their organization’s work ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).

### Twitter

- 17% of nonprofits worldwide have participated in or hosted a Tweet Chat ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- 77% of nonprofits worldwide have a Twitter Profile. 24% tweet two to five tweets daily, 21% less than one tweet weekly, 18% tweet once daily, 18% tweet one tweet every other day, 12% tweet once weekly, and 7% tweet five or more tweets daily ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Small nonprofits have an average of 4,241 Twitter Followers. Medium have 17,737. Large have 39,496 ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).

### Instagram

- 50% of NGOs worldwide have an Instagram Profile. 30% share less than once weekly, 24% share once weekly, 21% share once every other day, 17% share once daily, and 8% share twice or more daily ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Small nonprofits have an average of 1,837 Instagram Followers. Medium have 7,675. Large have 19,365 ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).

### **LinkedIn**

- 56% of NGO worldwide have a LinkedIn Page. 68% post less than once weekly, 15% post once weekly, 8% post once every other day, 6% post once daily, and 3% post twice or more daily ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
- Small nonprofits have an average of 785 LinkedIn Followers. Medium have 2,837. Large have 10,107 ([**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport-past-reports)).
