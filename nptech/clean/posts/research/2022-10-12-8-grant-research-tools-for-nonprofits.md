According to the [Giving USA 2022 Report](https://www.resilia.com/blog/highlights-from-the-giving-usa-foundation-2022-report), giving by foundations to nonprofit organizations grew by 3.4% in 2021 to $90.88 billion. Foundation grants now represent 19% of total giving to nonprofits – the largest share on record.

The websites listed below can help your nonprofit search and apply for grant funding. If you need training in grant writing, a good place to start is with this free [Introduction to Proposal Writing course](https://learning.candid.org/training/courses/introduction-to-proposal-writing/) and the [best-selling book, *How to Write a Grant*](https://www.learngrantwriting.org/learn-grant-writing-book/).

## 1. Candid :: [candid.org/find-funding](https://candid.org/find-funding)

Candid offers a searchable database of foundations and grants, grants for individuals, and Requests for Proposals published by funders.

## 2. Devex :: [devex.com/funding](https://pgs.devex.com/devex-pro-funding/)

Devex features international funding opportunities from 850+ sources including USAID, World Bank, the European Union, and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

[![Screenshot of devex.com Homepage - a funding tool for international development organizations.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/DEVEX-Pro-Funding-for-Nonprofits.jpg?resize=800%2C533)](https://pgs.devex.com/devex-pro-funding/)

[Plans & Pricing](https://pgs.devex.com/devex-pro-funding-orgs/#pricing-anchor)

## 3. Grant Gopher :: [grantgopher.com](https://grantgopher.com/)

Grant Gopher is a simple and powerful grant research database that nonprofits can search by state, county, program, and keywords.

[![Screenshot of grantgopher.org Homepage - a grantseeking tool for schools, nonprofits, and municipalities.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/09/Grant-Gopher-Homepage.jpg?resize=800%2C493)](https://grantgopher.com/#/)

[Plans & Pricing](https://grantgopher.com/Plans-Pricing)

## 4. Grants.gov :: [grants.gov](https://www.grants.gov/)

Grants.gov enables grant seekers to search for federal grants offered by the U.S. Government.

## 7. GrantWatch :: [grantwatch.com](https://www.grantwatch.com/)

GrantWatch allows users to filter searches by interest, location, funding source, keyword and/or Grant ID#.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/GrantWatch-for-Nonprofits.jpg?resize=800%2C547)](https://www.grantwatch.com/)

## **8. Instrumentl :: [instrumentl.com](https://www.instrumentl.com/)**

Instrumental is an online database of grant opportunities that matches nonprofits with the right funders and then enables nonprofits to efficiently track and manage their grants.

[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/Instrumental-for-Nonprofits.jpg?resize=800%2C503)](https://www.instrumentl.com/)

[Plans & Pricing](https://www.instrumentl.com/pricing)

In addition, for those nonprofits located in the United States, you can check with [regional philanthropy-serving organizations](https://www.unitedphilforum.org/find-your-regional-philanthropy-serving-organization) and your [state association of nonprofits](https://www.councilofnonprofits.org/find-your-state-association).
