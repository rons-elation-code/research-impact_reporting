# 2025 Online Fundraising Statistics for Nonprofits

> A supplemental post to 101 Digital Marketing & Fundraising Best Practices for Nonprofits, use these 50+ statistics to guide your nonprofit in creating and maintaining a successful online fundraising strategy.

A supplemental post to [101 Digital Marketing & Fundraising Best Practices for Nonprofits](https://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/), the statistics listed below can guide your nonprofit in creating and maintaining a successful online fundraising strategy.

---

## Online Fundraising Strategy

- 93% of nonprofits believe that a strong brand identity has a positive impact on donor engagement. [[99designs](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2022/07/16/infographic-how-good-design-impacts-nonprofit-digital-marketing-fundraising/)]
- 75% of donors look for concrete information about a nonprofit’s achievements before making a decision to give. [[DonorDirect](https://www.donordirect.com/blog/financial-management/donors-more-likely-to-give-after-seeing-tangible-results/)]
- 48% of donors cite email as their preferred method of hearing updates and appeals from the organization. Direct mail was the second most preferred channel at 21%, followed by social media (17%), text messaging (8%), and phone calls (2%). [[Neon One](https://neonone.com/resources/nonprofit-email-report/)]
- 72% of individuals say that the presence of a charity rating badge increases their likelihood of giving. [[Great Nonprofits](https://greatnonprofits.org/)]
- 84% of online donors are more likely to give if their donations are matched by their employer. [[Double the Donation](https://doublethedonation.com/)]
- The current donor retention rate is 41.9%. [[Fundraising Effectiveness Project](https://data.givingtuesday.org/fep-report/)]
- Frequent, consistent communication with online donors results in a 41.5% increase in revenue. [[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/blog/beginners-guide-to-donor-cultivation/)]
- The average return on ad spend (ROAS) for fundraising ads on Meta platforms is $0.50 USD. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]

## Online Giving

- The average gift made on desktop devices is $194; for mobile users, the average gift is $94. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 25% of online donations were made on a mobile device in 2022. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 30% of all online giving occurs in December and 10% occurs in the last three days of the year. [[Bonterra](https://www.bonterratech.com/)]
- 33% of online donors give tribute gifts to family and friends. Donors are most interested in giving tribute gifts as memorials (43%), birthday gifts (25%), and for religious holidays (10%)
- $222 is the median amount raised by one-time peer-to-peer fundraisers; $501 is the median amount raised by return fundraisers. [[Classy](https://www.classy.org/)]
- GivingTuesday 2023 raised $3.1 billion—only 0.6% higher than 2022—and 10% fewer people participated. [[GivingTuesday](https://www.givingtuesday.org/)]

## Monthly Giving

- The average monthly gift is $25. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Monthly giving accounts for 28% of all online revenue. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 16% of nonprofits use pop-ups to prompt one-time donors to become monthly donors. [[Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/)]
- ACH monthly donors are retained for 20% longer because there is no expiration date like credit cards. [[Classy](https://donationtrends.classy.org/)]

## Website Fundraising

- Visitors on desktop devices make up the majority of donation transactions (63%) and revenue (75%). [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 0.23% of organic website visitors made a donation, generating an average of $0.77 per visitor.[[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 68% of online donors most trust websites and email addresses that use the .org domain. [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- 50% of online donors will opt-in to pay credit card processing fees. [*[Classy](https://www.classy.org/blog/supporters-cover-donation-fees/)*]
- 72% of individuals say that the presence of a [charity rating badge](https://secure.kidneyfund.org/site/SPageNavigator/df_id/onetime75.html?s_src=mainform&s_subsrc=mainform_headeronetime&_ga=2.188730280.1481796608.1593991943-1802472686.1593991943) on a donation page increases their likelihood of giving.

## Email Fundraising

- 26% of donors say that email is the tool that most inspires them to give (25% social media, website 17%, print 13%). [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- 45% of online donors say that email is the communication method most likely to inspire repeat donations (25% social media, 13% print, 10% a handwritten note, 7% a phone call). [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- Email messaging results in 14% of all online revenue in 2022. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- For every 1,000 fundraising messages sent, nonprofits raised $90 in 2022.  [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Email revenue on Giving Tuesday declined by 18% in 2022. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Donor retention increases by 29% for offline donors when you have their email address. [[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/blog/complete-guide-email-acquisition/)]

## Social Media Fundraising

- Giving through Facebook Fundraising Tools resulted in 1.1% of all online revenue for nonprofits in 2022. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 89% of donors who have donated through Facebook Fundraising Tools say they are likely to do so again. [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- 93% of donors who have donated through Instagram Fundraising Tools say they are likely to do so again. [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- The average Facebook Fundraiser has four gifts that average $34 USD. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]

## Online Donors

- 54% of US households donate to charity. [[YouGov](https://today.yougov.com/society/articles/43435-half-americans-donate-money-charity-past-year-poll)]
- 51% of donations are made by women and 63% of GivingTuesday donations are made by women. [[Women’s Philanthropy Institute](https://scholarworks.iupui.edu/server/api/core/bitstreams/ae8f3d6d-5cce-4a82-b5f5-3d3d750e9894/content)]
- 88% of the Silent Generation gives to charity, donating an annual average of $1,367 across 6.2 organizations. [[AARP](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/08/04/6-generations-of-giving-who-gives-the-most-and-how-they-prefer-to-give/)]
- 72% give to charity, donating an annual average of $1,212 across an average of 4.5 organizations. [[AARP](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/08/04/6-generations-of-giving-who-gives-the-most-and-how-they-prefer-to-give/)]
- Gen X donates an average annual gift of $732 across four charities and leads in annual volunteer hours served. [[Nonprofit Pro](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/08/04/6-generations-of-giving-who-gives-the-most-and-how-they-prefer-to-give/)]
- 84% give to charity, donating an annual average of $481 across 3.3 organizations. [[AARP](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/08/04/6-generations-of-giving-who-gives-the-most-and-how-they-prefer-to-give/)]
- 8% of donors give stock or mutual funds to nonprofits and 4% donate to Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs). [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- 66% of donors worldwide volunteer with nonprofits. Of those, 73% donate to the nonprofits they volunteer for. [[Global Trends in Giving Report](http://www.nptechforgood.com/global-trends-in-giving-report/)]
- Affluent households give an average of $34,917 to charity. [[Bank of America Study of Philanthropy](https://philanthropy.iupui.edu/news-events/news/_news/2023/Charitable%20Giving%20by%20Affluent%20Households%20Above%20Pre-Pandemic%20Levels,%20Finds%202023%20Bank%20of%20America%20Study%20of%20Philanthropy.html)]
