A supplemental post to [101 Digital Marketing & Fundraising Best Practices for Nonprofits](https://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/), the email marketing statistics listed below can guide your nonprofit in launching and maintaining an email marketing strategy that engages supporters and inspires donors.

---

## Email Strategy

- 86% of nonprofits utilize email marketing. Of those, 45% send newsletters monthly, 24% quarterly, 13% weekly, 9% twice monthly, 6% every two months, 2% twice weekly, and 1% send daily. [[2025 Nonprofit Tech for Good Survey](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2025/04/15/announcing-the-2025-nonprofit-tech-for-good-survey/)]
- Nonprofits sent an average of 62 email messages per subscriber in 2024, a 9% increase in volume from the previous year. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- 63% of nonprofits use personalization in their email marketing. [[Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/)]
- Emails with personalized subject lines are 26% more likely to be opened. [[Campaign Monitor](https://www.campaignmonitor.com/resources/glossary/personalization/)]
- Personalized calls to action convert 202% better than default calls to action. [[HubSpot](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/personalized-calls-to-action-convert-better-data)]
- 17% use email subscribe popups on their website. [[2025 Nonprofit Tech for Good Survey](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2025/04/15/announcing-the-2025-nonprofit-tech-for-good-survey/)]
- Only 13% of nonprofits are using gated content to grow their email list. [[Nonprofit Communications Trends Report](https://www.nonprofitmarketingguide.com/the-nonprofit-communications-trends-report/)]
- 81% of marketers use generative AI for email creation. Of those, 85% report that AI improved content quality. [[HubSpot](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/state-of-generative-ai?hubs_content=blog.hubspot.com/marketing/email-marketing-stats&hubs_content-cta=HubSpot%27s%20State%20of%20Generative%20AI)]

## Email Engagement

- Nonprofits have an average email list size of 4,191 contacts (547 for small nonprofits and 6,602 for large nonprofits). [[Neon One Nonprofit Email Report](https://neonone.com/resources/nonprofit-email-report/)]
- Nonprofits have an average open rate of 28.59% and an average click rate of 3.29%. [[Neon One Nonprofit Email Report](https://neonone.com/resources/nonprofit-email-report/)]
- The average open rate for welcome emails is 80%. [[Mailmodo](https://www.mailmodo.com/guides/welcome-email-statistics/)]
- In 2024, 9% of subscribers unsubscribed and 7% became non-deliverable due to bouncing. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Email list sizes increased by 4% in 2024 – a decline from 6% growth in 2023. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Only 35% of nonprofits delete unengaged subscribers on a regular basis. [[2025 Nonprofit Tech for Good Survey](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2025/04/15/announcing-the-2025-nonprofit-tech-for-good-survey/)]
- 94% of nonprofits that utilize email marketing use an email marketing service, such as MailChimp or Constant Contact. The remaining 6% send their email campaigns using BCC. [[Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/)]

## Email Fundraising

- 33% of donors say that email is the tool that most inspires them to give (29% social media, website 17%, print 9%). [[2025 Online Donor Feedback Survey](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2025/02/15/the-communication-giving-preferences-of-641-online-donors/)]
- Of the 86% of nonprofits that utilize email marketing, 76% send email fundraising appeals and of those 66% send quarterly, 14% every two months, 12% monthly, 4% twice monthly, 3% weekly, and 1% daily. [[2025 Nonprofit Tech for Good Survey](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2025/04/15/announcing-the-2025-nonprofit-tech-for-good-survey/)]
- 48% of donors cite email as their preferred method of hearing updates and appeals from the organization. Direct mail was the second most preferred channel at 21%, followed by social media (17%), text messaging (8%), and phone calls (2%). [[Neon One Nonprofit Email Report](https://neonone.com/resources/nonprofit-email-report/)]
- The average nonprofit raises $1.11 per email contact – $6.15 for small nonprofits, $0.88 for large nonprofits. [[Neon One Nonprofit Email Report](https://neonone.com/resources/nonprofit-email-report/)]
- 35% of those nonprofits that send email fundraising appeals raised the amount of money that they expected, 34% raised more, and 31% raised less than they expected. [[Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/)]
- Email messaging resulted in 11% of all online revenue in 2024 – down from 14% in 2023. [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- For every 1,000 fundraising messages sent, nonprofits raised $58 in 2024.  [[M+R Benchmarks](https://mrbenchmarks.com/)]
- Donor retention increases by 29% for offline donors when you have their email address. [[NextAfter](https://www.nextafter.com/blog/complete-guide-email-acquisition/)]

## Email Design

- 53% of email is opened on a mobile device and if an email is not designed for mobile, it’s likely to be deleted in under three seconds and as many as 15% of users will unsubscribe. [[Campaign Monitor](https://www.campaignmonitor.com/blog/email-marketing/7-stats-that-will-make-you-rethink-mobile-email/)]
- 35% of nonprofits use emojis in email, 18% animated GIFs, 12% polls, and 6% countdown timers. [[Nonprofit Communications Trends Report](https://www.nonprofitmarketingguide.com/the-nonprofit-communications-trends-report/)]
- Including videos in emails can boost click-through rates by 65%. [[Campaign Monitor](https://www.campaignmonitor.com/resources/knowledge-base/what-is-the-average-ctr-from-videos/)]
- Emails with graphics have a 1.12% higher click-through rate than emails without any. [[Unlayer](https://unlayer.com/blog/email-graphics)]
- Countdown timers in emails can boost registrations by 30%. [[HubSpot](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/interactive-email?_ga=2.103694019.21785958.1639976361-1824751501.1619072512)]
- Subject lines comprising 6 to 10 words achieve the highest open rate at 21%. [[MailModo]](https://www.mailmodo.com/guides/email-subject-line-statistics/)
- Email subject lines with questions have a 50% higher open rate and subject lines with numbers have a 17% higher open rate. [[MailModo]](https://www.mailmodo.com/guides/email-subject-line-statistics/)
- 35% of nonprofits use emojis in subject lines. [[Nonprofit Communications Trends Report](https://www.nonprofitmarketingguide.com/the-nonprofit-communications-trends-report/)]
