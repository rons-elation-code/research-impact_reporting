# AI Resources for Nonprofits

**[AI Hub for Nonprofits](https://www.nptechforgood.com/ai-for-nonprofits/) > [Articles](https://www.nptechforgood.com/ai-for-nonprofits/articles/) > [Podcasts](https://www.nptechforgood.com/ai-for-nonprofits/podcasts/) > [Tools](https://www.nptechforgood.com/ai-for-nonprofits/tools/) > Resources**

• [AI4NGO](https://www.ai4ngo.org/) provides NGOs worldwide with the AI tools and training necessary to increase their impact and address critical global and local challenges.

• [The Marketing Artificial Intelligence Institute](https://www.marketingaiinstitute.com/) offers free webinars, a podcast, a comprehensive weekly newsletter, and blog content about how AI is shaping marketing, politics, and economics.

• [AI for Nonprofits](https://www.ai4np.org/) is a shared knowledge exchange to help nonprofits ethically and effectively adopt AI.

• [The AI Impact Hub](https://aiimpacthub.com/) is a community and course ecosystem that provides practical AI skills, ongoing learning, and peer community.

• [Fundraising.ai](https://fundraising.ai/) is independent collaborative that exists to understand and promote the development and use of Responsible and Beneficial Artificial Intelligence for Nonprofit Fundraising.

• [The OpenAI Academy for Nonprofits](https://academy.openai.com/home/clubs/nonprofits-8kc1e/overview?linkMenu=Nonprofits) offers resources and tutorials to equip nonprofit professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to harness artificial intelligence effectively.

• [Google for Nonprofits](https://gettimeback.withgoogle.com/more) offers free AI tools for fundraising, marketing, or program management.

• [The Microsoft Digital Skills Center for Nonprofits](https://page.techsoup.org/digital-skills-center) is a collaboration between TechSoup and Microsoft that offers AI training to enhance your marketing, donor relations, research capabilities, and fundraising.

• [UNESCO’s Ethics of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/recommendation-ethics) is the first-ever global standard on AI ethics (released in September 2021). It’s cornerstone is the protection of human rights and dignity.
