# 10 Website Marketing Best Practices for Nonprofits

> Your website is the foundation upon which your digital marketing and fundraising campaigns are built. How well your nonprofit’s email and social media campaigns perform depends upon how well your website communicates your mission and programs and whether it is optimized to capture leads and inspire action.

Your website is the foundation upon which your digital marketing and fundraising campaigns are built. How well your nonprofit’s email and social media campaigns perform depends upon how well your website communicates your mission and programs and whether it is optimized to capture leads and inspire action.

## **1)** Study and implement modern design trends.

Large national and international nonprofits are often ahead of the curve. They have the resources to hire and consult highly skilled website design agencies and graphic designers. In the early stages of a website redesign for your nonprofit, spend a few hours studying the websites of large nonprofits similar to yours in mission and programs. Note what you like and dislike, take notice of the [color scheme](https://webflow.com/blog/web-design-101-color-theory), how the navigation works, and what your eyes are first drawn to. Having a vision for your next website design is very important so that you can communicate your needs clearly to those involved in the redesign process.

A Google search of “[website design trends 2026](https://www.google.com/search?q=website+design+trends+2026&sca_esv=588736867&sxsrf=AE3TifMIEEi3pXCexggYKO1G_bCkcIwGrw%3A1766236375186&ei=16BGaaWUC9bdkPIPsrzcOA&ved=0ahUKEwjl2-2dn8yRAxXWLkQIHTIeFwcQ4dUDCBE&uact=5&oq=website+design+trends+2026&gs_lp=Egxnd3Mtd2l6LXNlcnAiGndlYnNpdGUgZGVzaWduIHRyZW5kcyAyMDI2MgUQABiABDIIEAAYogQYiQUyCBAAGKIEGIkFSMkZUOAFWN0IcAF4AZABAJgBjAGgAeQBqgEDMS4xuAEDyAEA-AEBmAIDoALvAcICChAAGLADGNYEGEfCAgoQIxiABBgnGIoFwgIGEAAYFhgemAMAiAYBkAYIkgcDMi4xoAflCrIHAzEuMbgH6wHCBwUwLjEuMsgHCIAIAA&sclient=gws-wiz-serp)” adds an extra layer of creativity to your process and ensures that by the time of launch, your website will include innovative and forward-thinking design elements.

Nonprofits would be well-served to [optimize their website for those with visual and hearing disabilities](https://torchbox.com/charity/blog-charity/guide-to-website-accessibility-for-charities/) to expand their reach and when relevant, expand their website to include more than one language.

## 2) Use a top-rated Content Management System (CMS).

58% of nonprofits use [WordPress.org](https://wordpress.org/) as their content management system (CMS) for their website (7% use Wix, 6% use Squarespace, and 5% use Drupal), according to the [Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/). Released in 2003, WordPress is a free open-source software for websites and blogs that can be customized for nonprofits by using [WordPress themes](https://themeforest.net/category/wordpress/nonprofit/charity) and [plugins](https://nonprofitwp.org/select-plugins/recommended-wordpress-plugins-nonprofits/). The website for Nonprofit Tech for Good is built on WordPress and in our 16 years of publication, the performance of WordPress has been excellent.

That said, most nonprofit marketing professionals do not know how to build a website using WordPress, nor do they have the graphic design skills necessary to redesign a website, but there are [freelancers for hire](https://www.upwork.com/) and most website design agencies have WordPress expertise. The cost ranges from $5,000 to $100,000 USD depending upon your graphic design needs, the complexity of the site, and how many work hours are required.

[![WordPress promotional page with text highlighting its open-source platform. Sections labeled Design, Build, and Extend showcase features with related images.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/WordPress-for-Nonprofits-e1741884114852.png?resize=800%2C461&ssl=1)](https://wordpress.org/)

For those nonprofits on a tiny budget, there are [do-it-yourself website builders](https://www.pcmag.com/roundup/334639/the-best-website-builders), but a better option is website template platforms built specifically for nonprofits, such as [Wired Impact](https://wiredimpact.com/designs/) and [FireSpring](https://www.firespring.com/services/nonprofit-websites/index.html). If you’re looking for a website design agency specifically for nonprofits, you can start your research by contacting [CONCAT](https://concat.tech/), [Constructive](https://constructive.co/), [BCS Interactive](https://bcsinteractive.com/), and [Elevation](https://www.elevationweb.org/).

Finally, during the process of hiring a freelance website designer or agency, ensure that they have the skill set to integrate your website with your customer relationship manager (CRM) and any other third-party services (MailerLite, PayPal, etc.) that your nonprofit uses.

## **3)** Prominently feature a “Donate” button and email opt-in on every page of your website.

Many nonprofits deprioritized using their website to grow their email list during the rise of social media and that was a mistake. Organic reach on social media is at an all-time low, so it no longer makes sense to prominently feature social media icons on your website.

First and foremost, your “Donate” button should be included in your website’s primary navigation so that it is prominently featured on every page of your website. The button should be large and colorful so that it is obvious and not easily missed.

[![NRDC homepage featuring a young girl in a coral shirt touching clear water, with an adult nearby. Bold text reads 'Be a Force for the Future' with a signup form.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NRDC-Homepage-e1741884072183.png?resize=800%2C495&ssl=1)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NRDC-Homepage-e1741884072183.png?ssl=1)

Next, add a newsletter opt-in to your website. The [Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC)](https://www.nrdc.org/) knows the value of growing their email list and features an email opt-in on their homepage and on every page of their website by featuring an email opt-in in the footer of their website.

[![NRDC footer with donation options, email signup for the Activist Network, and navigation links to About, Resources, Media Center, Careers, Contact US, and small social media icons.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NRDC-Footer-e1741884092336.png?resize=800%2C501&ssl=1)](https://www.nrdc.org/)

Finally, it’s worth noting the very small social media icons featured in the lower right of the [NRDC’s footer](https://www.nrdc.org/) **↑**. Their placement speaks to the decrease in the power of social media for nonprofits. Most large national and international nonprofits no longer prominently feature social media icons on their website.

## **4)** Maximize your website’s sidebar.

Nonprofit Tech for Good receives more website traffic from our sidebar than from our primary navigation bar, yet most nonprofits underutilize their sidebars.

Think of your website’s sidebar as free advertising space and utilize the space for important calls to action. [The Pancreatic Cancer Action Network](https://pancan.org/news/the-current-state-of-federal-funding-for-pancreatic-cancer-research-a-call-to-action/) excels in its use of a sidebar in its “News” section and features a support line, a newsletter opt-in, and call to follow on social media.

[![A blog post on the website for PANCAN that highlight an email newsletter opt-in and social network icons in the right bar.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Pancreatic-Action-Network-side-bar-on-website-e1701967458950.png?resize=800%2C634&ssl=1)](https://pancan.org/news/the-current-state-of-federal-funding-for-pancreatic-cancer-research-a-call-to-action/)

## **5)** Prioritize easy-to-read text and CTAs, visually compelling images, and provide ample white space.

It’s important that your website not overwhelm visitors. Easy-to-read text and calls to action combined with visually compelling images that are padded with [white space](https://getflywheel.com/layout/effective-use-white-space-web-design/) are defining traits of modern website design.

The [Nature Conservancy](https://www.nature.org/en-us/) uses white space to focus a visitor’s attention on news and important CTAs. It’s an acquired skill to know when less content is more in terms of website design.

## **6)** Embrace simplicity in navigation.

With [62% of global website traffic occurring on mobile devices](https://www.statista.com/statistics/277125/share-of-website-traffic-coming-from-mobile-devices/), simplicity in navigation is essential. A good example is [Birdlife International](https://www.birdlife.org/) which lists navigation links at the top of their website, rather than drop-downs, making it easy for website visitors to browse their website on desktop *and* mobile screens.

[![BirdLife International homepage featuring a close-up of blue parrot feathers with bold text 'SAVE PARROTS NOW' and a green 'DONATE NOW' button for conservation.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Birdlife-International-Homepage-e1741987509886.png?resize=800%2C419&ssl=1)](https://www.birdlife.org/)

[![Mobile navigation menu for BirdLife International website with a blue background, white text links for Home, About, How We Work, News, Advocacy, Our Science, Support Us, Become a Member, and a search icon.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Birdlife-International-on-Mobile-e1742136754830.png?resize=350%2C548&ssl=1)](https://www.birdlife.org/)

If your nonprofit needs to use drop-downs to organize a large amount of website content, the [National Parks Conservation Association](https://www.npca.org/) does so effectively. Drop-down links are in a single column which helps visitors quickly focus and easily navigate their website.

[![National Parks Conservation Association homepage featuring a scenic park view, a dropdown menu under 'Issues,' and a call-to-action box about saving the park system.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NPCA-Home-Page-e1741988639358.png?resize=800%2C557&ssl=1)](https://www.npca.org/)[![Mobile navigation menu for the National Parks Conservation Association website, showing sections like Issues, Parks, Our Impact, and Get Involved.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NPCA-Navigation-on-Mobile-e1742136779682.png?resize=350%2C564&ssl=1)](https://www.npca.org/)

## **7)** **Use a social sharing app to customize how your website content appears on social media (if necessary).**

[Social sharing buttons are rarely used](https://www.seocomponent.com/blog/you-dont-need-social-share-buttons-on-your-website/) and they can slow your website, so in most cases it’s best to not add social sharing buttons to your website. However, using a social sharing app, such as [Social Snap](https://socialsnap.com/), allows website editors to easily customize the title, description, and image that is autogenerated when a page on your website is posted on social media.

Too often, website pages on a nonprofit’s website autogenerate incorrect text and irrelevant images when posted on social media which decreases engagement and click-throughs. A good social sharing app provides complete control over how your website content posts on social media without having to add social sharing buttons to your website.

## 8) Invest in premium website hosting.

According to the [Nonprofit Tech for Good Report](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/02/01/announcing-the-2023-nonprofit-tech-for-good-report/), 27% of nonprofits worldwide have experienced a cyberattack, such as email phishing, website hacking, ransomware, etc. For nonprofits, investing in a premium website hosting service that offers increased security and automatic backups is becoming non-negotiable. Budget website hosting is tempting for small nonprofits on a limited budget, but eventually, the high price of using low-cost website hosting becomes painfully obvious.

For the first ten years of publication, Nonprofit Tech for Good used budget website hosting for $12 a month. By 2020, our website downtime had become significant and our email opt-in forms had become overrun with spam bot subscribers, so we [upgraded to premium hosting via Flywheel](https://getflywheel.com/pricing/). Since then, our website has experienced no downtime, bot email subscribers continue to be blocked, and customer service is exceptional.

The investment in premium website hosting also improved our website speed. In [Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/), Nonprofit Tech for Good immediately improved from a failing score of 64 to a passed score of 92. Increased website speed makes Nonprofit Tech for Good easier to use for our readers and has boosted our search engine optimization (SEO). If your nonprofit prioritizes SEO in its [digital marketing and fundraising strategy](https://www.nptechforgood.com/earn-a-certificate-in-digital-marketing-fundraising-from-nonprofit-tech-for-good/), then investing in premium website hosting is a must.

## 9) Optimize your website for Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO).

As email click-through rates and organic reach on social media continue to decline, SEO is more important than ever for increasing referral traffic to your website from Google Search, [ChatGPT Search](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-search/), and [Microsoft Bing](https://www.nptechforgood.com/2023/05/14/an-introduction-to-bing-tools-for-nonprofits/). To optimize your website for SEO, follow these steps:

1. Install [Google Analytics](https://analytics.google.com/) on your website and sign up for [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/about) to get access to detailed website traffic information and Google Search performance. In addition, your nonprofits should create a [Bing Webmaster Tools](https://www.bing.com/webmasters/about) account to monitor and increase referral traffic from Bing.
2. Install an [SSL certificate](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/ssl/what-is-an-ssl-certificate/) on your website. Google began classifying websites without an SSL certificate as “Not Safe” on July 1, 2019 and no longer displays them in search results.
3. Use a premium website hosting service to improve the speed of your website. Google Search and Microsoft Bing de-prioritize slow-loading websites in their search results.
4. Publish new content to your website on a regular basis, such as a [blog or news section](https://www.nptechforgood.com/101-best-practices/10-blogging-best-practices-for-nonprofits/), because search engines crawl and prioritize websites that create new content.
5. Use your nonprofit’s [keywords](https://yoast.com/what-is-a-keyword/) consistently in titles and in the body of blog posts and news articles.
6. Subscribe to [Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/newsletter) to receive [SEO Best Practices](https://backlinko.com/hub/seo/best-practices) via email.

Nonprofit Tech for Good is diligent about monitoring our SEO and [GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)](https://www.semrush.com/blog/generative-engine-optimization/), and in 2025, [ChatGPT Search became a top source of referral traffic](https://taktical.co/blog/google-traffic-is-down-heres-how-to-get-website-traffic-with-ai-search/) likely due to our overall SEO performance, our content strategy, and the 13-year history of our domain (nptechforgood.com).

## **10)** Add a privacy and cookie policy to your website.

Nonprofit Tech for Good can not offer legal advice, but if your nonprofit collects information from website visitors via web forms and [cookies](https://www.adpushup.com/blog/types-of-cookies/) (such as Google Analytics, the Facebook Pixel, autofill for donation forms, etc.), then you need to add a privacy and cookie policy to your website. A good example to follow is [American Wild Horse Conservation](https://americanwildhorse.org/) which features a cookie popup in the lower left of their website that links to their [privacy and cookie policy](https://americanwildhorse.org/legal/privacy-policy).

[![Homepage of American Wild Horse Conservation with bold text stating 'America’s Voice for Wild Horse & Burro Conservation,' a 'Get Involved' signup form, and a cookie and privacy policy opt-in.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/American-Wild-Horse-Conservation-Homepage-e1742145799813.png?resize=800%2C441&ssl=1)](https://americanwildhorse.org/)

If your nonprofit uses WordPress, then you can [use a cookie consent plugin](https://blog.hubspot.com/website/cookie-consent-plugin) to easily add a cookie banner to your website that is legally compliant with numerous data privacy laws worldwide.

If your nonprofit needs a privacy policy, start by studying the privacy policies posted on the websites of large nonprofits. In some cases, you can copy and tweak. To write your own privacy, [use a policy generator](https://app.privacypolicies.com/) or consult with a lawyer.

[Data privacy laws are expanding worldwide](https://www.osano.com/articles/data-privacy-laws) and cookie and privacy policy banners and opt-ins have become a must-have for all nonprofits.
