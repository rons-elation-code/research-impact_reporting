# The Top 3 Charity Rating & Badge Programs for Nonprofits

> Seventy-two percent of individuals say that the presence of a charity rating badge increases their likelihood of giving. If your nonprofit does not have charity rating badges on your website, then it’s time to look into getting registered with Charity Navigator, Candid, and GreatNonprofits. All three programs are free to use, accept online donations, and are nonprofit organizations themselves.

**By [Heather Mansfield](http://www.nptechforgood.com/heather-mansfield/), founder and editor-in-chief of Nonprofit Tech for Good**

---

Seventy-two percent of individuals say that the [presence of a charity rating badge increases their likelihood of giving](https://www.toprated.greatnonprofits.org/get-started/galleryPage). If your nonprofit does not have charity rating badges on your website, then it’s time to look into getting registered with Charity Navigator, Candid, and GreatNonprofits. All three programs are free to use, accept online donations for your nonprofit, and are nonprofit organizations themselves.

It’s worth noting that Charity Navigator, Candid, and GreatNonprofits are only available to nonprofits based in the United States. If your nonprofit is located outside of the US, then Google “charity ratings [your country name]” to find similar programs.

## 1) Charity Navigator :: [charitynavigator.org](https://www.charitynavigator.org/)

Founded in 2002, Charity Navigator currently provides information about the financial health, transparency, and accountability of more than 1.6 million nonprofits in the United States and has issued ratings for nearly 225,000 501(c)(3) tax-exempt charitable organizations. To begin, visit their [FAQ for Nonprofits](https://www.charitynavigator.org/about-us/faqs/) and then [conduct a search for your nonprofit on Charity Navigator](https://www.charitynavigator.org/discover-charities/) to see if your nonprofit has been rated. To update your profile and access your badge, sign up to access the [Nonprofit Portal](http://charitynavigator.org/portal).

Eligible charities receive a zero to four-star star rating which is determined by the weighted sum of your nonprofit’s [beacon scores (0-100)](https://www.charitynavigator.org/about-us/our-methodology/ratings/).

[![A visual break down of Charity Navigator rating - by score and stars.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Charity-Navigator-Ratings.jpg?resize=778%2C424&ssl=1)](https://www.charitynavigator.org/about-us/our-methodology/ratings/)

For example, Action Against Hunger has earned a [near-perfect score of 99% score from Charity Navigator](https://www.charitynavigator.org/ein/133327220), thus a ★ ★ ★ ★ rating, and placed their badge near the “Donate Now” button [on their website and donation form pop-ups](https://www.actionagainsthunger.org/).

[![screenshot of the home page of Action Against Hunger nonprofit website with charity badges near their "Donate Now" button.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Action-Against-Hunger-Home-Page-e1713970351993.png?resize=800%2C461&ssl=1)](https://www.actionagainsthunger.org/)

## **2) Candid :: [candid.org](https://candid.org/)**

Candid (formerly known as Guidestar) released the first-ever searchable database of nonprofits in 1996 and since then has built one of the most comprehensive, up-to-date databases available to donors, nonprofits, and foundations worldwide. Through their [APIs](https://candid.org/use-our-data/apis), more than 200 websites that serve the nonprofit sector, such as [Global Giving](https://www.globalgiving.org/) and [Benevity](https://benevity.com/) use Candid in order to quickly validate a nonprofit’s legal status and contact information.

To begin, [sign up to update your nonprofit’s Candid profile](https://www.guidestar.org/UpdateNonprofitProfile). You’re nonprofit will then earn a [Seal of Transparency](https://www.guidestar.org/UpdateNonprofitProfile?search=false#seals-of-transparency) based on the level of information provided.[![A visual breakdown of the Seals of Transparency offered by Candid.org for nonprofits.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Candid-Seals-of-Transparency.jpg?resize=623%2C859&ssl=1)](https://www.guidestar.org/UpdateNonprofitProfile?search=false#seals-of-transparency)A clever use of a Candid Seal of Transparency is the [Warrior Canine Connection](https://twitter.com/WarriorCanineCn/status/1761141599252181177) which occasionally promotes and embeds its seal on social media graphics.

[![A tweet on X featuring a picture of a labrador retriever for veterans with PTSD with a Candid Platinum Seal embedded on top.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Warrior-Canine-Connection-e1713971105350.png?resize=500%2C716&ssl=1)](https://twitter.com/WarriorCanineCn/status/1761141599252181177)

## 3) GreatNonprofits :: [greatnonprofits.org](https://greatnonprofits.org/)

Launched in 2007, GreatNonprofits is unique from Charity Navigator and Candid in that their charity rating badges are awarded based on reviews from your donors and supporters. Any nonprofit that gathers 10 or more positive reviews and maintains an overall average of 3.5+ stars from January 1 to December 31 each year earns a [GreatNonprofits Top-Rated award](https://www.about.greatnonprofits.org/greatnonprofits-badge). It’s worth noting that GreatNonprofits reviews also appear on Candid.

To begin, [sign up to claim and set up your nonprofit’s profile](https://www.toprated.greatnonprofits.org/get-started/galleryPage) and then invite your supporters, donors, and volunteers to write a review. Once you have earned your badge, add it to your nonprofit’s website. For example, [Friends of Saguaro National Park](https://greatnonprofits.org/org/friends-of-saguaro-national-park-inc) is a small nonprofit located in Tucson, AZ that has featured its GreatNonprofits badge on the home page of its website.

[![The home page of Friends of Saguaro National Park that features a GreatNonprofits badge.](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Friends-of-Saguaro-National-Park-2023-e1678808269627.jpg?resize=800%2C641&ssl=1)](https://www.friendsofsaguaro.org/)
