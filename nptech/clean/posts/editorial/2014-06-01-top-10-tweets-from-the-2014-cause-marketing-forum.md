# Top 10 Tweets From the 2014 Cause Marketing Forum

> Thanks to a partnership with the Public Interest Registry, Heather Mansfield of Nonprofit Tech for Good was able to report live from the Cause Marketing Forum (Storified) last week in Chicago, IL. When reviewing the hundreds of tweets using the #CMF2014 hashtag, one obvious theme emerged – tweets with images are retweeted significantly more often than text-only tweets. The founder of Twitter himself, Evan Williams, has said that influence on Twitter is not primarily in follower count, but rather in how often a user is retweeted. [![Top 10 Tweets from Cause Marketing Forum](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Top-10-Tweets-from-Cause-Marketing-Forum-300x300.jpg?resize=300%2C300)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Top-10-Tweets-from-Cause-Marketing-Forum.jpg)Thanks to a partnership with the [Public Interest Registry](http://pir.org/), [Heather Mansfield](http://www.nptechforgood.com/heather-mansfield/) of Nonprofit Tech for Good was able to report live from the [Cause Marketing Forum (Storified)](https://storify.com/nonprofitorgs) last week in Chicago, IL. When reviewing the hundreds of tweets using the [#CMF2014 hashtag](https://twitter.com/search?q=%23CMF2014&src=typd), one obvious theme emerged – tweets with images are retweeted significantly more often than text-only tweets. The founder of Twitter himself, Evan Williams, has said that influence on Twitter is not primarily in follower count, but rather in how often a user is retweeted. So, for [your nonprofit’s next conference](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/05/04/how-to-report-live-from-nonprofit-events-and-conferences/), prioritize using images.
