# 12 Early Adopters of the .NGO and .ONG Domains

> Unlike the .ORG domain which can be registered by any individual or brand (but mostly registered by nonprofits and socially conscious for-profits), the new .NGO and .ONG domains can only be registered by nonprofits, charities, NGOs (non-governmental organizations), and ONGs (non-governmental organizations in a romance-based language). Think about that for a second or two. [![Early Adopter NGOs ONGs Domains Facebook](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Early-Adopter-NGOs-ONGs-Domains-Facebook-300x300.jpg?resize=300%2C300)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Early-Adopter-NGOs-ONGs-Domains-Facebook.jpg)Unlike the .ORG domain which can be registered by any individual or brand (but mostly registered by nonprofits and socially conscious for-profits), the **[new .NGO and .ONG domains](https://www.ongood.ngo/portal/registrars)** can only be registered by nonprofits, charities, NGOs (non-governmental organizations), and ONGs (non-governmental organizations in a **[romance-based language](http://globalngo.org/world-maps-ngo-and-ong-usage-around-the-globe/)**). *Think about that for a second or two*. When your online donors and advocates see that your nonprofit uses .NGO or .ONG for your website URL and email addresses, there will be no doubt in their minds that your nonprofit is legal ***because your nonprofit must complete a [validation process](https://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/19470129505/in/album-72157655116691039/) via [OnGood](https://www.ongood.ngo/portal/) to be able to use the domain*s**. In an online world where anyone or any brand can launch online fundraising campaign through third-party crowdfunding sites (such as [**GoFundMe**](https://www.gofundme.com/) and **[Indiegogo](http://www.indiegogo.com)**), it will become increasingly important to communicate to online donors and advocates that your nonprofit is legal, valid, and an online brand to be trusted.

That said, below is a list of early adopters using the new .NGO and .ONG domains. Current growth is occurring in parts of the world where “NGO” and “ONG” are commonly used terms and in regions where “nonprofit” and “charity” are more common, many nonprofits and charities are purchasing the domains (they are sold as a bundle) and having them forward to their .ORG website(s).
