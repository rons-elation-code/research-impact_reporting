[![Web Design Nonprofit FB](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/Web-Design-Nonprofit-FB-300x300.png?resize=300%2C300)](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/Web-Design-Nonprofit-FB.png)The average person worldwide has five social media accounts and spends an average of **[1 hour and 40 minutes](http://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/newsbysector/mediatechnologyandtelecoms/11610959/Is-your-daily-social-media-usage-higher-than-average.html)** browsing these networks every day. In 2005 Facebook was only open to college students and professors, Twitter didn’t exist, and the iPhone was still in concept mode. The Internet was primarily a desktop PC or laptop experience and reading news and sending and receiving email accounted for the majority of time spent online.

Today, social media consumes the most time spent on the Internet and the majority of that browsing occurs on a smartphone or tablet. Also, over the last decade the amount of time we spend on the Internet has quadrupled. That’s radical change in a relatively short amount of time and it directly corresponds with the rapid rise of social media worldwide.

Nonprofits pioneered social media. They began actively using Myspace in 2004 and YouTube in 2005 – years before the Fortune 500 companies. And yet despite limited budgets, the **[early adopter nonprofits began using responsive web design in 2012](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2012/10/08/three-nonprofits-pioneering-responsive-web-design/)** in order to adapt quickly to the new realities of Internet browsing. Bold imagery. Large font. Simplified, tappable navigation and calls-to-action. Dramatic use of color. Social, shareable content. These website design trends appeal to today’s Internet users on every device, from PC to smartphone, and are required to stand out from the tens of millions of brands active every day on social media. All that said, below are ten nonprofits embracing **[responsive web design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Responsive_web_design)** in 2015 **([view 2014 list](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2014/02/25/11-responsively-designed-nonprofit-websites-to-study-and-learn-from/))** compared with their 2005 website designs.

Written on the premise that all communications and fundraising are now mobile and social, Mobile for Good is a step-by-step how-to guide for writing, implementing, and maintaining a mobile and social fundraising strategy for your nonprofit.
