## 1. The Mobile Web will rule by 2014.

- As of September 2013, [91% of American adults own a cellphone](http://www.pewinternet.org/Reports/2013/Cell-Activities.aspx), [61% own a smartphone](http://www.comscore.com/Insights/Press_Releases/2013/10/comScore_Reports_August_2013_US_Smartphone_Subscriber_Market_Share), and [34% own a tablet](http://www.pewinternet.org/Reports/2013/Tablet-Ownership-2013.aspx).
- [63% of American adults now access the Mobile Web daily](http://www.pewinternet.org/Reports/2013/Cell-Internet.aspx) through mobile browsers and smartphone/tablet apps.
- [In 2014](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8358799172/in/photostream), it is projected that the Mobile Web will surpass desktop.
- **Priority #1:** [Launch a new website](http://www.bureauforgood.com/) that is [responsively designed](http://www.nptechforgood.com/2013/04/07/nonprofitresponsivedesign/).

## **2. Mobile email is on the rise.**

- As of September 2013, [47% of email is now opened on a mobile device](http://thenextweb.com/dd/2013/09/17/why-responsive-email-design-is-more-important-than-ever-2/).
- In 2012 [online donations via email dropped 21%](http://www.e-benchmarksstudy.com/) due to [donate pages not being mobile compatible](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8657553157/).
- **Priority #2:** [Mobile optimize your donate page](https://www.donationpay.org/site/) and [redesign your e-newsletter](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8359512816/in/photostream) to ensure that is compatible with mobile email

## **3. Social networks have gone mobile.**

- As of February 2013, [65% of social networking occurs on a mobile device](http://mashable.com/2013/10/24/content-consumption-desktop-mobile/) especially during commutes, lunch breaks, and evening hours.
- Your nonprofit would be wise to act upon the fact that the majority of your fans and followers [access the content you post on social networks on mobile devices](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8657555707/).
- **Priority #3:** Purchase a [smartphone](http://cell-phones.toptenreviews.com/smartphones/) and [tablet](http://tablets-review.toptenreviews.com/) for your communications and development staff.

## 4. Texting is a tool *still* not mastered.

- One trillion text messages sent globally in 2008. 10 trillion sent by the end of 2011.
- Mobile phone owners sent an average of 678 texts a month
- The majority of the nonprofits that are pioneering texting and/or text-to- give do not have mobile websites. (View [CDC Text](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8363986351/in/photostream), [CDC Mobile Website](http://www.flickr.com/photos/nonprofitorgs/8363989201/in/photostream))
- **Priority #4:** [Start collecting mobile phone numbers](http://www.peta.org/) and launch a texting and/or text-to-give campaign.

## **5. Mobile giving is the future.**

- [Mobile/digital wallet technology](http://www.google.com/wallet/) will transform online and mobile fundraising. (View [Hills for Everyone](http://www.hillsforeveryone.org/donate/index.html), [Urgent Text Fundraising Appeal](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2011/11/photo2.png))
- **Priority #5:** Download and acquaint yourself with mobile wallets. [Google Wallet](http://www.google.com/wallet/) is a good starting point. Google Wallet donate buttons are already featured on [YouTube channels](http://www.youtube.com/bigcatrescue) and will likely be featured soon on [Google+ Pages](https://plus.google.com/+bigcatrescue/posts) as well.
