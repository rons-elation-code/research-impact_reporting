[![](https://i0.wp.com/www.nptechforgood.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Tweet-Chat-NGOtech19.jpg?resize=600%2C300)](https://www.facebook.com/events/1296986543838673/)

Join Nonprofit Tech for Good, **[Funraise](https://www.facebook.com/funraiseplatform/)**, and the [**2019 Global NGO Technology Report partners**](https://funraise.org/techreport-partners) for a 24-hour Twitter Takeover on September 26! You can participate and follow the takeovers on Twitter at [**@NonprofitOrgs**](https://twitter.com/nonprofitorgs).

Please review the schedule below and then join, discuss, share, and follow at [**#NGOtech19**](https://twitter.com/hashtag/ngotech19):

– Ask our experts about NGO technology trends to watch for in 2020.  
– Share your expertise and commentary about technology best practices and challenges happening in your country.  
– Share success stories and examples of good use of technology by NGOs, NPOs, and charities.  
– Share resources, such as ebooks, blog posts, and articles about NGO technology that you think would be useful to the followers of the Twitter Takeover.

### Schedule of Twitter Takeovers on September 26:

10am AEST (Melbourne): [**Pro Bono Australia**](https://probonoaustralia.com.au/)  
9:30am IST (Ahmedabad): [**NGOBOX**](https://ngobox.org)  
9:30am WAT (Lagos): [**Nigeria Network of NGOs**](https://nnngo.org/)  
11am IST (Dublin): [**Digital Charity Lab**](https://www.digitalcharitylab.org/)  
1:30pm CEST (Paris): **[Webassoc](https://www.webassoc.org/)**

9am PDT (Long Beach): [**Funraise**](https://funraise.org/)

For help with time zones, [**RSVP on Facebook**](https://www.facebook.com/events/1296986543838673/) or use **[TimeandDate.com](https://www.timeanddate.com/)**.

The [**Global NGO Technology Report**](https://funraise.org/techreport) is a biennial research project that seeks to gain a better understanding of how non-governmental organizations (NGOs) worldwide use web and email communications, online fundraising tools, social media, mobile technology, and productivity software. Now in its fourth edition, this year’s report provides technology benchmarks for Africa, Asia, Australia and New Zealand, Europe, Latin America and the Caribbean, and the United States and Canada.
