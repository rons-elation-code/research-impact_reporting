# HOW TO: Add YouTube’s New InVideo Programming to Your Nonprofit’s Videos

> Seemingly less than 1% of nonprofit have upgraded to YouTube’s new One Channel design, but even fewer appear to be utilizing YouTube’s new InVideo Programming option. If you haven’t spent any time recently exploring YouTube’s enhanced tool set, I’d highly recommend that you set aside an hour or two to upgrade to the new design, add InVideo Programming, and merge your YouTube Channel with your nonprofit’s Google+ Page. That said, to see examples of InVideo Programming and to learn how to add InVideo Programming to your nonprofit’s videos, simply follow the steps below: Step 1: Go to your Channel Settings >

[![videographic](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/videographic.png?w=300&resize=300%2C207)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/videographic.png)Seemingly less than 1% of nonprofit have upgraded to [YouTube’s new One Channel design](http://www.youtube.com/onechannel), but even fewer appear to be utilizing YouTube’s new InVideo Programming option. If you haven’t spent any time recently exploring YouTube’s enhanced tool set, I’d highly recommend that you set aside an hour or two to upgrade to the new design, add InVideo Programming, and [merge your YouTube Channel with your nonprofit’s Google+ Page](http://nonprofitorgs.wordpress.com/2013/05/05/how-to-merge-your-nonprofits-youtube-channel-and-google-page/). That said, to see examples of InVideo Programming and to learn how to add InVideo Programming to your nonprofit’s videos, simply follow the steps below:

**Step 1: Go to your Channel Settings > InVideo Programming.**

[![YouTube Settings 2](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-2.png?resize=600%2C157)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-2.png)

[![YouTube Settings](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings1.png?resize=600%2C356)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings1.png)

**Step 2: Select “Feature your channel” to add your avatar to all your videos.***Choose position and select your display time.*

[![YouTube Settings 3](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-3.png?resize=600%2C286)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-3.png)

**View Example: [Peta Video](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ql8xkSYvwJs) [Warning: Graphic Video]**

**Note: When clicked the YouTube viewer can subscribe to Peta’s Channel.**

[![Peta Video](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/peta-video.png?resize=600%2C364)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/peta-video.png)

**Step 3: Or Select “Feature a video” to add a video to all your  videos.***Choose video and select position, your start and display time, and duration.*

[![select video](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/select-video.png?resize=600%2C390)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/select-video.png)

[![YouTube Settings 4](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-4.png?resize=600%2C298)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-4.png)

**View Example: [Anaheim Ballet (Starts at 00:10)](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bQ0L24YbGGI)**

**Note: When clicked the YouTube viewer can watch your selected video.**

[![YouTube Settings 8](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-81.png?resize=600%2C365)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube-settings-81.png)
