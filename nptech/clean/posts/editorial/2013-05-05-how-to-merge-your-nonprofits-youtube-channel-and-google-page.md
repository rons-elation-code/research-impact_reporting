[![YouTube](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube.jpg?w=300&resize=300%2C168)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube.jpg)Google’s strategy of making Google+ the thread that links all [Google Products](http://www.google.com/about/products/) together is working. Google+ can now lay claim to [359 million active monthly users](http://www.businessinsider.com/google-plus-is-outpacing-twitter-2013-5) and the title of being the world’s second largest social network. If your nonprofit uses Gmail, Search, YouTube, Maps, etc., then you can’t avoid having a presence on Google+.

Currently in Beta, YouTube just announced the ability to merge your YouTube channel with your nonprofit’s Google+ page. After merging the two you can then upload videos to your YouTube channel directly from your Google+ page, access both your channel and page with one login, and a new “YouTube” tab will appear on your Google+ page.  To begin the merging process, your [YouTube channel must already have a personal Google+ profile added as a manager](http://youtubecreator.blogspot.com/2013/04/using-google-page-identity-on-youtube.html) and then follow the seven steps detailed below:

**Step 1: Go to your YouTube Settings > Advanced.**

[![YouTube1](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube11.png?resize=600%2C370)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube11.png)

**Step 2: Select “Connect with a Google+ page.**

[![YouTube2](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube21.png?resize=600%2C370)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube21.png)

**Step 3: Select your nonprofit’s Google+ page.**

[![YouTube3](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube3.png?w=600&resize=600%2C271)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube3.png)

**Step 4: Confirm that you want to merge your channel and your Google+ page.**

[![YouTube4](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube4.png?w=600&resize=600%2C344)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube4.png)

**Step 5: Note the new YouTube tab on your Google+ page.**

[![YouTube55](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube551.png?w=600&resize=600%2C344)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube551.png)

**Step 6: Select “Use YouTube as [your nonprofit].”**

[![YouTube6](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube6.png?resize=600%2C373)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube6.png)

**Step 7: Note the integration of of your Google+ page inside your YouTube channel.**

[![YouTube7](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube7.png?resize=600%2C370)](http://nonprofitorgs.files.wordpress.com/2013/05/youtube7.png)
