# 1. Blockchain – [#blockchainforgood](https://twitter.com/hashtag/blockchainforgood)

A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed and public digital ledger that is used to record transactions across many computers so that the record cannot be altered retroactively without the alteration of all subsequent blocks and the collusion of the network. – [**Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockchain)

# 2. Cryptocurrency – [#cryptoforgood](https://twitter.com/hashtag/cryptoforgood)

A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. A cryptocurrency is difficult to counterfeit because of this security feature. A defining feature of a cryptocurrency, and arguably its most endearing allure, is its organic nature; it is not issued by any central authority, rendering it theoretically immune to government interference or manipulation. – [**Investopedia**](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/c/cryptocurrency.asp)

# 4. Encryption – **[#encryptionforgood](https://twitter.com/hashtag/encryptionforgood)**

Data encryption translates data into another form, or code, so that only people with access to a secret key (decryption key) or password can read it. – [**Digital Guardian**](https://digitalguardian.com/blog/what-data-encryption)

# 5. Internet of Things – [#iotforgood](https://twitter.com/hashtag/iotforgood)

The Internet of things (IoT) is the network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, actuators, and connectivity which enables these objects to connect and exchange data. – **[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_of_things)**
